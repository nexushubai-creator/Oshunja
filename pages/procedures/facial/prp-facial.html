Overview

The PRP Facial at OSHUNJA combines microneedling with platelet-rich plasma (PRP) extracted from your own blood. This powerful treatment stimulates collagen production, improves skin texture, reduces fine lines, and creates a natural glow—using your body's own healing growth factors.

In Kingston's sun-rich environment, skin can show premature aging from UV exposure. Our PRP facials are particularly effective for Caribbean skin, addressing concerns like hyperpigmentation, sun damage, and uneven tone while promoting cellular regeneration and healing.

The treatment involves drawing a small amount of blood, isolating the concentrated platelets, then applying this serum to your skin during microneedling. The result is accelerated healing, enhanced collagen synthesis, and remarkable skin transformation over the following months.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Blood Draw & PRP Preparation

A small amount of blood (similar to routine lab work) is drawn from your arm. The sample is placed in a centrifuge to separate and concentrate the platelet-rich plasma containing powerful growth factors.

2

Skin Preparation & Numbing

Your face is thoroughly cleansed and a topical numbing cream is applied for 20-30 minutes. We want you comfortable throughout the microneedling portion of the treatment.

3

Microneedling with PRP Application

Using a sterile microneedling device, we create thousands of tiny micro-channels in the skin. Your concentrated PRP is simultaneously applied and pressed deep into the dermis, where growth factors stimulate collagen and healing.

4

Post-Treatment Care & Recovery

Any remaining PRP is applied as a serum to the skin surface. We provide detailed aftercare instructions, and you'll leave with some redness—like a mild sunburn—that fades within 2-3 days.

5

Series & Follow‑Up

Most patients complete 3–4 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart. We reassess progress and plan maintenance for lasting glow and collagen support.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

PRP Facial May Be Right for You If:

  • You're noticing early signs of aging—fine lines, dullness, uneven texture—and want to stimulate natural rejuvenation
  • You have sun damage, hyperpigmentation, or melasma from Jamaica's tropical climate and want a natural approach to evening skin tone
  • You prefer natural, biocompatible treatments over synthetic chemicals or foreign substances
  • You're willing to undergo a series of 3-4 treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart for optimal results
  • You can commit to 3-5 days of downtime with visible redness and some peeling
  • You're looking for gradual, progressive improvement rather than instant transformation
  • You're in good health with no active skin infections, blood disorders, or conditions affecting healing

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Blood Disorders: Certain clotting disorders, platelet dysfunction, or conditions requiring blood thinners may make PRP unsafe—we'll review your medical history carefully
  • Active Skin Conditions: If you have active acne, rosacea flares, eczema, or infections at treatment sites, we'll need to postpone until skin is clear
  • Sun Exposure: You must avoid sun exposure and tanning for at least 2 weeks before and after treatment—difficult but essential in Kingston's sunny climate
  • Realistic Timeline: PRP works gradually through natural collagen remodeling over 2-3 months—it's not a quick fix like injectables
  • Multiple Sessions: Most patients need 3-4 treatments for optimal results, so factor this commitment and cost into your planning
  • Post-Treatment Care: Expect 2-3 days of redness and possible peeling—plan around social events and work obligations
Contraindications: PRP Facial is not recommended for those with blood clotting disorders, active skin infections, cancer, immunosuppression, or certain chronic skin conditions. We'll perform a thorough evaluation during consultation.

Recovery & Aftercare

First 24 Hours

  • Expect moderate redness similar to a sunburn
  • Some swelling and tenderness is normal
  • Avoid touching or rubbing your face
  • Sleep with head elevated to minimize swelling
  • Use only gentle, approved cleansers if needed

Days 2-3

  • Redness begins to fade significantly
  • Skin may feel tight or dry—avoid harsh products
  • Light peeling or flaking may occur (don't pick!)
  • Resume gentle skincare routine as directed
  • Most patients return to work at this point

First Week

  • Visible redness completely resolves
  • Skin texture begins to smooth and glow
  • Continue sun protection religiously
  • Avoid makeup for first 24-48 hours, then use mineral-based products
  • Resume normal activities while protecting skin

4-6 Weeks

  • Noticeable improvement in texture, tone, and fine lines
  • Collagen production accelerates during this period
  • Schedule next treatment in your series
  • Continue medical-grade skincare as recommended

Aftercare Instructions

Sun Protection: Critical! Use SPF 50+ and avoid direct sun for 2 weeks in Jamaica's climate
Skincare: Use only gentle, non-irritating products for first week—no retinols, acids, or exfoliants
Hydration: Drink plenty of water and use a gentle, hydrating moisturizer
Activity: Avoid intense exercise for 24-48 hours to prevent excessive sweating and inflammation
Heat: No saunas, hot tubs, or steam rooms for one week
Makeup: Wait 24-48 hours, then use clean mineral-based products only
When to Contact Us: Call if you experience severe or worsening pain, signs of infection (increasing redness, warmth, pus), allergic reaction, or unusual symptoms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How soon can I travel after liposuction 360?

Plan to remain in Kingston for at least seven days for follow-ups and lymphatic care. This ensures optimal healing and allows our team to monitor your progress during the critical early recovery period.

When can I return to work?

Desk work usually resumes in 7 to 10 days; more active roles may need three weeks. We provide work notes as needed and will guide you based on your specific job requirements and healing progress.

Do I have to wear compression?

Yes. Continuous compression is vital for shaping and controlling swelling for six weeks. The compression garment supports your new contours and significantly improves your final results.

Will fat come back?

Removed fat cells do not return, but remaining cells can grow if weight increases, so maintenance is key. With a stable weight and healthy lifestyle, your results will be long-lasting.

How much fat can be removed safely?

Safety guidelines typically limit removal to 5 liters or about 5% of body weight. Our surgeons prioritize your safety and will determine the appropriate amount based on your anatomy and health status.

Is liposuction 360 painful?

Most patients describe moderate discomfort rather than pain, easily managed with prescribed medication. The tumescent technique used helps minimize post-operative discomfort.

Can this replace diet and exercise?

No, this is not a weight-loss procedure. It's designed for body contouring and removing stubborn fat pockets that resist diet and exercise. Best results come when you're near your goal weight.

What about scarring?

Incisions are tiny (3-5mm) and strategically placed to be inconspicuous. With proper care, they fade to barely visible marks within 6-12 months.

How much does liposuction 360 cost in Jamaica?

The cost varies depending on the extent of treatment and whether it's combined with other procedures. During your consultation, we'll provide a detailed quote. We offer flexible payment plans to make treatment accessible.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Absolutely. Our surgeons have extensive experience treating patients of all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. We use techniques specifically tailored to optimize healing for darker skin types.
