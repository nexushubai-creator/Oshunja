AFTERCARE

Recovery & Aftercare Instructions

Immediate Care (First 24 Hours)

  • Do not lie down for 4 hours after treatment
  • Avoid touching or massaging the treated areas
  • Skip strenuous exercise for 24 hours
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours
  • Stay upright and avoid bending over
  • No facials or massage for 24 hours

First Week After Treatment

  • Avoid heat exposure (saunas, hot yoga, sun)
  • Use sunscreen daily to protect treated skin
  • Sleep on your back if possible
  • Stay hydrated to support healing
  • Be patient - results take 3-5 days to appear

QUESTIONS?

Frequently Asked Questions About Botox

How long does Botox last?

Botox typically lasts 3-4 months. With regular treatments, some patients find their results last longer as muscles become trained to relax. We recommend scheduling maintenance treatments every 3-4 months for optimal results.

Does Botox hurt?

Most patients describe the sensation as a quick pinch or slight stinging. We use ultra-fine needles to minimize discomfort. Ice or numbing cream can be applied if you're particularly sensitive, though most patients don't require it.

Will I look frozen or unnatural?

Not when administered by our experienced physicians. We use precise dosing and strategic placement to smooth wrinkles while maintaining natural facial expressions. You'll look like yourself - just refreshed and rejuvenated.

What's the difference between Botox and fillers?

Botox relaxes muscles to smooth dynamic wrinkles caused by facial expressions. Fillers (like Juvederm) add volume to restore lost fullness and smooth static wrinkles. Often, they're used together for comprehensive facial rejuvenation.

How much does Botox cost in Kingston, Jamaica?

Botox pricing varies based on the number of treatment areas and units required. During your complimentary consultation, we'll provide a detailed quote tailored to your needs. We offer flexible payment plans to make treatment accessible.

When will I see results?

You'll begin to notice results in 3-5 days as the Botox takes effect. Full results are visible at 2 weeks. We schedule a complimentary follow-up at this time to ensure you're completely satisfied with your results.

Is Botox safe for darker skin tones?

Absolutely. Botox works on muscles, not skin pigmentation, making it safe and effective for all skin tones including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. Our physicians have extensive experience treating diverse patients in Kingston.

Can I get Botox if I'm planning pregnancy?

We do not recommend Botox during pregnancy or breastfeeding as a precaution, though studies haven't shown adverse effects. If you're planning to become pregnant, discuss timing with your physician.

What age should I start Botox?

Many patients start "preventative Botox" in their mid-to-late 20s or early 30s to prevent deep wrinkles from forming. Others wait until lines are more visible. The ideal age depends on your skin, genetics, and personal goals.

Can Botox treat excessive sweating?

Yes! Botox is FDA-approved to treat hyperhidrosis (excessive sweating) in the underarms, hands, and feet. If you struggle with excessive perspiration, ask about this treatment during your consultation.
