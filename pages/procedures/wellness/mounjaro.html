Overview

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) represents the latest breakthrough in weight management, offering even more powerful results than Ozempic. This dual-action GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist works through two complementary pathways to reduce appetite, slow digestion, and promote significant weight loss—with clinical trials showing up to 22% body weight reduction over 72 weeks when combined with lifestyle modifications.

At OSHUNJA in Kingston, we offer medically supervised Mounjaro treatment as part of a comprehensive weight management program. Our team provides thorough evaluation, personalized dosing, nutritional counseling, and ongoing monitoring to maximize your results while minimizing side effects. For patients who need more aggressive weight loss or haven't achieved desired results with Ozempic, Mounjaro offers a more potent solution.

Beyond weight loss, Mounjaro improves metabolic health markers including blood sugar control, insulin sensitivity, and cardiovascular risk factors. Our holistic approach ensures you receive the education, support, and medical oversight necessary for safe, sustainable transformation at our Kingston clinic.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Mounjaro May Be Right for You If:

  • You need more aggressive weight loss than Ozempic provides—Mounjaro delivers superior results in clinical trials
  • You have a BMI of 27+ with weight-related conditions (diabetes, hypertension, sleep apnea) or BMI 30+ without comorbidities
  • You've plateaued on Ozempic or other GLP-1 medications and want to try the most powerful option available
  • You're committed to lifestyle changes including nutrition and activity—medication alone won't get you there
  • You're seeking physician-supervised weight loss treatment with comprehensive medical monitoring in Kingston
  • You can commit to weekly self-injections and regular follow-ups for dose adjustments and progress tracking
  • You understand this is long-term treatment—stopping often leads to weight regain, so plan accordingly

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Family Planning: Not safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Stop Mounjaro at least 2 months before attempting conception
  • Thyroid Cancer History: Contraindicated if you or family members have medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN 2 syndrome
  • Pancreatitis Risk: History of pancreatitis requires careful evaluation—Mounjaro may increase risk in susceptible patients
  • Side Effects: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea are common, especially during dose escalation. Usually improve but can be more intense than Ozempic
  • Cost Considerations: Mounjaro is typically more expensive than Ozempic. Insurance coverage varies—we'll help navigate options
  • Long-Term Commitment: Weight often returns when stopping. Plan for sustained treatment to maintain results—this is a lifestyle medication

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mounjaro more effective than Ozempic?

Yes. Clinical trials show Mounjaro produces greater weight loss—up to 22% body weight reduction compared to Ozempic's 15%. The dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism provides enhanced appetite suppression and metabolic benefits.

What are the side effects of Mounjaro?

Most common are GI-related: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and decreased appetite. Side effects are typically more pronounced than Ozempic but usually improve after the first few weeks. We provide management strategies.

How much does Mounjaro cost in Jamaica?

Cost varies significantly depending on insurance coverage. Mounjaro is typically more expensive than Ozempic. Our Kingston team can provide current pricing and help navigate insurance options or alternative payment plans.

Can I switch from Ozempic to Mounjaro?

Yes, many patients transition to Mounjaro if they've plateaued on Ozempic or want more aggressive weight loss. We'll guide the transition, typically starting with a lower Mounjaro dose to minimize side effects.

How long until I see results?

Most patients notice appetite reduction within the first week. Weight loss becomes visible after 4-8 weeks. Maximum results typically occur around 6-12 months with proper adherence to treatment and lifestyle modifications.

Will I regain weight if I stop Mounjaro?

Many patients regain some or all weight after stopping. Mounjaro addresses the biological mechanisms that make weight loss difficult, so discontinuing removes that support. Plan for long-term treatment for sustained results.

Can I use Mounjaro for diabetes?

Yes! Mounjaro is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes and provides superior blood sugar control. It's often prescribed off-label for weight loss in non-diabetic patients. Your indication may affect insurance coverage.

What's the injection schedule?

Once weekly, same day each week. Start at 2.5mg for 4 weeks, then increase to 5mg. Dose can be escalated every 4 weeks up to maximum 15mg based on tolerance and results. We guide you through the process.

Are there food restrictions on Mounjaro?

No specific restrictions, but we recommend protein-rich, nutrient-dense foods with adequate hydration. Smaller, more frequent meals help manage reduced appetite and minimize GI side effects. Our dietitian provides personalized guidance.

Can men use Mounjaro for weight loss?

Absolutely! Mounjaro is equally effective for men and women. The medication works the same regardless of gender, and we treat many male patients at our Kingston clinic seeking weight loss or diabetes management.
