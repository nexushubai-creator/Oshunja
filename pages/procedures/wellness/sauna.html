Overview

Infrared sauna therapy at OSHUNJA uses penetrating infrared heat (120-140°F) to promote deep detoxification through sweat, reduce inflammation, accelerate muscle recovery, improve cardiovascular health, and enhance relaxation. Unlike traditional saunas that heat the air around you, infrared wavelengths penetrate up to 1.5 inches into tissue, warming you from the inside out for superior therapeutic benefits with greater comfort.

In Kingston's tropical climate, our climate-controlled infrared sauna provides a tranquil escape that supports whole-body wellness. Whether you're an athlete seeking recovery, managing chronic pain or inflammation, pursuing detoxification, or simply looking to reduce stress and improve sleep, regular sauna sessions deliver scientifically-backed benefits that complement your health goals.

Sessions last 30-45 minutes at comfortable temperatures where you can relax, meditate, or listen to music while your body undergoes profound healing. The gentle heat causes deep sweating that flushes toxins, improves circulation, burns calories, and triggers beneficial stress responses that strengthen your cardiovascular system. Our private sauna rooms ensure a peaceful, restorative experience tailored to your wellness needs.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Pre-Session Preparation

Arrive well-hydrated (drink 16-20oz water before session). Wear comfortable, light clothing or bring a towel. Our staff will explain sauna operation, safety protocols, and optimal session duration based on your experience level.

2

Initial Warmup (5-10 minutes)

Enter the private infrared sauna as it reaches optimal temperature (120-140°F). Sit or lie comfortably while infrared heat begins penetrating tissue. You'll feel gentle warmth building gradually—much more comfortable than traditional saunas.

3

Deep Heat Session (20-35 minutes)

Relax as infrared penetrates deep into muscles and tissue. Deep sweating begins as your core temperature rises slightly, triggering detoxification and cardiovascular benefits. Breathe deeply, meditate, or enjoy provided entertainment. Staff checks periodically to ensure comfort.

4

Cool Down & Rehydrate

Exit when your target time is reached or if you feel uncomfortable. Towel off sweat, change if needed, and rest briefly in our cooling area. Rehydrate immediately with electrolyte-enhanced water to replace fluids lost through sweating.

5

Post-Session Recovery

Many clients report immediate relaxation, reduced muscle tension, and mental clarity. Detoxification continues for hours post-session. Schedule your next session (2-4 per week recommended) and track benefits over time with our Kingston wellness team.

During The Session

  • Infrared penetrates tissue for deeper therapeutic effects
  • Private room ensures peaceful, uninterrupted relaxation
  • Exit anytime if you feel dizzy, nauseous, or overheated

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Infrared Sauna May Be Right for You If:

  • You're an athlete seeking faster recovery from training, reduced muscle soreness, and enhanced performance
  • You struggle with chronic pain from arthritis, fibromyalgia, old injuries, or autoimmune inflammation
  • You want natural detoxification support to eliminate environmental toxins, heavy metals, and metabolic waste
  • You're managing stress, anxiety, or sleep issues and want a scientifically-backed relaxation tool
  • You have cardiovascular goals—sauna therapy supports heart health, circulation, and blood pressure management
  • You're seeking skin health improvements—clearer complexion, better tone, anti-aging collagen benefits
  • You're in generally good health with no contraindications to heat exposure
  • You're willing to commit to regular sessions (2-4 per week) for optimal cumulative benefits

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Cardiovascular Conditions: Heart disease, uncontrolled hypertension, or recent cardiac events require physician clearance before sauna use
  • Pregnancy: We don't recommend sauna therapy during pregnancy due to risks of elevated core temperature to the fetus
  • Medications: Diuretics, blood pressure meds, or medications causing heat sensitivity may require dosage adjustments—consult your doctor
  • Acute Illness: Avoid sauna if you have fever, active infection, or acute inflammatory conditions until recovered
  • Hydration Critical: Drink 16-20oz water before and after sessions—dehydration risk increases with sweating
  • Listen to Your Body: Exit immediately if you feel dizzy, nauseous, lightheaded, or develop rapid heartbeat
Contraindications: Infrared sauna is not recommended for those with severe cardiovascular disease, uncontrolled hypertension, pregnancy, recent surgery, or conditions causing heat intolerance. Always consult your physician before beginning sauna therapy.

Session Guidelines & Best Practices

Before Your Session

  • Hydrate thoroughly—drink 16-20oz water 30-60 minutes before
  • Avoid heavy meals for 1-2 hours prior (light snack okay)
  • Skip alcohol or caffeine which can cause dehydration
  • Bring towel, light clothing, and water bottle
  • Remove jewelry, glasses, contact lenses if desired

During Your Session

  • Start with 20-30 minutes if you're new to sauna therapy
  • Sit or lie comfortably—change positions if needed
  • Breathe deeply and relax—meditate, listen to music
  • Wipe sweat periodically to promote continued sweating
  • Exit immediately if you feel dizzy, nauseous, or uncomfortable

After Your Session

  • Cool down gradually—don't rush into cold shower
  • Rehydrate immediately with 16-24oz water or electrolyte drink
  • Shower to rinse off toxins released through sweat
  • Rest 15-20 minutes before resuming strenuous activity
  • Continue drinking water throughout the day

Optimal Frequency

  • Beginners: Start with 1-2 sessions per week, 20-30 minutes each
  • Regular users: 2-4 sessions per week, 30-45 minutes each
  • Athletes/recovery: Daily sessions okay with proper hydration
  • Build tolerance gradually—don't push through extreme discomfort

Maximizing Benefits

Timing: Best post-workout or evening before bed for recovery and sleep
Hydration: Critical before, during (if needed), and after sessions
Consistency: Regular sessions (2-4/week) deliver best cumulative results
Pairing: Combine with cold plunge for contrast therapy benefits
Nutrition: Support detox with clean diet rich in antioxidants
Rest: Allow 15-20 minutes post-session before intense activity
When to Stop: Exit immediately if you experience chest pain, severe dizziness, difficulty breathing, rapid/irregular heartbeat, or feel faint. Contact medical staff if symptoms persist.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I use the infrared sauna?

Most people benefit from 2-4 sessions per week, 30-45 minutes each. Athletes may use it daily during heavy training. Beginners should start with 1-2 sessions weekly at shorter durations (20-30 minutes) and build tolerance gradually. Consistency matters more than frequency for long-term benefits.

What's the difference between infrared and traditional saunas?

Traditional saunas heat the air around you (170-200°F), while infrared saunas use infrared wavelengths to heat your body directly at lower temperatures (120-140°F). Infrared penetrates deeper into tissue for superior detoxification and therapeutic benefits while being more comfortable and tolerable.

Is sauna therapy safe if I have high blood pressure?

If your blood pressure is well-controlled with medication and your physician approves, sauna may actually help lower blood pressure over time. However, uncontrolled hypertension is a contraindication. Always consult your cardiologist before beginning sauna therapy if you have cardiovascular conditions.

Can sauna therapy help me lose weight?

Sauna sessions burn 300-600 calories by increasing heart rate and metabolism, but this isn't a standalone weight loss solution. Sweating causes temporary water weight loss that returns with rehydration. Sauna is best used as part of comprehensive program including nutrition, exercise, and lifestyle changes.

Should I sauna before or after working out?

After is ideal. Post-workout sauna accelerates recovery by improving circulation and reducing inflammation. Pre-workout sauna may decrease performance due to dehydration and elevated core temperature. Always rehydrate thoroughly between workout and sauna session.

How much water should I drink with sauna therapy?

Drink 16-20oz water 30-60 minutes before your session. Rehydrate with 16-24oz immediately after, plus additional water throughout the day. You'll lose 1-2 pounds of water weight per session through sweat, so adequate hydration is critical for safety and benefits.

Can I use sauna if I'm pregnant?

No. We don't recommend sauna therapy during pregnancy due to risks of elevated core body temperature to fetal development. This applies to all stages of pregnancy. Wait until after delivery and postpartum recovery to resume sauna sessions.

What should I wear in the infrared sauna?

Most clients wear swimsuit, athletic shorts, or light clothing. Some prefer towel only for maximum skin exposure to infrared. Bring a towel to sit on and wipe sweat. Remove jewelry, glasses, and anything metal that might heat up. Comfort is key.

Will sauna help with my chronic pain or arthritis?

Yes! Infrared heat therapy is excellent for chronic pain conditions. It increases circulation, reduces inflammation, relaxes muscles, and stimulates endorphin release. Many arthritis, fibromyalgia, and chronic pain sufferers report significant relief with regular sauna use (2-4x weekly).

Can I do sauna and cold plunge in the same session?

Absolutely! Contrast therapy (alternating hot sauna and cold plunge) provides enhanced benefits—better circulation, immune stimulation, and recovery. Typical protocol: 15-20 min sauna, 2-3 min cold plunge, repeat 2-3 cycles, end with cold. This is our most popular wellness combination.
