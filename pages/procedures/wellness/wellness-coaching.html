Overview

Wellness coaching at OSHUNJA provides personalized guidance and accountability to help you achieve lasting health transformation in Kingston, Jamaica. Whether you're navigating weight loss, managing chronic conditions, improving fitness, reducing stress, or simply optimizing overall wellbeing, our coaches provide the structure, support, and strategies you need to succeed where you've struggled before.

Unlike quick fixes or one-size-fits-all programs, wellness coaching addresses the whole person—nutrition, movement, sleep, stress, mindset, and habits. We help you identify barriers, develop sustainable routines, and build skills for long-term success. This isn't about perfection; it's about progress and finding what actually works for your life in Kingston.

Your coach serves as your partner, accountability buddy, and expert guide. Through regular sessions (in-person or virtual), you'll set realistic goals, track progress, celebrate wins, troubleshoot challenges, and continuously refine your approach. Real, lasting change happens with support—let us be that support for you.

How It Works & What To Expect

Wellness coaching provides structured support, accountability, and expert guidance to help you achieve sustainable health transformation. Our process is designed to meet you where you are and build lasting skills for lifelong wellness.

1

Initial Wellness Assessment

Your first session (60-90 minutes) is a comprehensive evaluation of your current health status, lifestyle, goals, challenges, and motivation. We explore your health history, typical daily routines, eating patterns, physical activity, sleep quality, stress levels, previous attempts at change, and what's worked or failed before. This deep understanding allows us to create a truly personalized coaching plan—not generic advice.

2

Goal Setting & Vision Creation

Together we establish clear, specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) goals. Instead of vague aspirations like "be healthier," we define concrete targets: lose 15 pounds in 3 months, reduce A1C from 6.8 to 6.2, walk 10,000 steps daily, sleep 7+ hours nightly. We also explore your deeper "why"—the emotional drivers that will sustain you when motivation wanes.

3

Personalized Action Plan Development

Based on your assessment and goals, we create a customized action plan addressing nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress management, and mindset. Plans start with 1-3 small, manageable changes to build momentum and confidence. We don't overwhelm you with dramatic life overhauls—we focus on sustainable habits that compound over time.

4

Regular Coaching Sessions

Meet with your coach weekly or bi-weekly (30-60 minutes) in person at our Kingston facility or via secure video call. Sessions include progress review, celebrating wins, identifying obstacles, problem-solving challenges, adjusting strategies, learning new skills (meal prep, stress reduction techniques, exercise form), and planning for upcoming challenges (travel, holidays, stressful periods).

5

Accountability & Tracking

Between sessions, track relevant metrics: weight, body measurements, food logs, activity levels, sleep hours, energy ratings, mood, or symptoms. Your coach reviews this data to identify patterns, celebrate consistency, and spot areas needing adjustment. Accountability isn't about judgment—it's about awareness and support. Knowing someone is checking in dramatically improves adherence.

6

Behavior Change Techniques

Learn evidence-based strategies for lasting habit formation: habit stacking, environment design, implementation intentions ("if-then" planning), overcoming self-sabotage, managing emotional eating, building intrinsic motivation, reframing setbacks as learning opportunities, and developing self-compassion. These skills serve you long after coaching ends.

7

Collaborative Care Coordination

Your coach works alongside your physician, dietitian, and other healthcare providers to ensure integrated care. We communicate progress, share relevant data, and adjust plans based on medical guidance. If you're on medications, managing conditions, or receiving other treatments, coaching complements your medical care—not replaces it.

8

Graduation & Maintenance Planning

Once you've achieved your primary goals and demonstrated consistent habits for 8-12 weeks, transition to maintenance phase with less frequent check-ins (monthly or quarterly). We develop strategies for sustaining progress independently, recognizing warning signs of relapse, and knowing when to re-engage support if needed. Long-term success is the ultimate goal.

Complementary Wellness Services

Wellness coaching amplifies the effectiveness of other health interventions by providing the behavioral support and accountability needed to implement recommendations consistently. Coaching integrates seamlessly with medical and wellness treatments.

Dietetics & Nutritional Counseling

Your dietitian creates the meal plan; your wellness coach helps you actually follow it consistently. Coaching addresses emotional eating, meal planning overwhelm, social eating challenges, and motivation issues that prevent dietary adherence. The combination produces far better nutrition outcomes than dietetics alone.

Metabolic Health Testing

Testing reveals WHAT needs to change (high glucose, poor lipids, nutrient deficiencies); coaching helps you make those changes happen. Your coach uses lab results to set concrete targets and track objective improvements over time, providing motivation through measurable progress.

GLP-1 Medications (Ozempic/Mounjaro)

Medications reduce appetite and support weight loss, but lasting success requires lifestyle changes. Coaching helps you establish healthy eating patterns, increase activity, manage stress, and build habits that sustain weight maintenance after medication ends—preventing relapse.

Hormone Optimization Therapy

Hormone optimization improves energy, mood, and metabolism—but you still need to eat well, exercise, sleep, and manage stress. Coaching helps you capitalize on the improved physiological state created by hormone therapy through strategic lifestyle optimization.

IV Nutrient Therapy

IV therapy provides acute nutrient replenishment and energy boosts, while coaching addresses the dietary, lifestyle, and behavioral factors causing chronic nutrient depletion. Together they correct deficiencies acutely and prevent recurrence long-term.

Sauna, Cold Plunge & Recovery Modalities

Recovery modalities support physical wellness, but consistency is key. Coaching helps you build routines that incorporate regular sauna sessions, cold exposure, stretching, and self-care practices—turning occasional visits into sustainable recovery habits.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I meet with my wellness coach?

Most clients meet with their coach weekly or bi-weekly (every 2 weeks) for optimal accountability and support. Initial phases typically benefit from weekly sessions to establish momentum and address early challenges. As habits solidify and you gain confidence (usually after 8-12 weeks), many clients transition to bi-weekly or monthly check-ins. Session frequency is customized based on your goals, progress, and preferences.

Are sessions in-person or virtual?

Both options are available! You can meet with your coach in-person at our Kingston facility or via secure video call from anywhere. Virtual coaching is just as effective as in-person—studies show similar outcomes. Many clients prefer virtual for convenience and flexibility, while others value face-to-face connection. You can also mix both formats based on your schedule.

How long does wellness coaching last?

Most clients engage in coaching for 3-6 months initially to establish sustainable habits and achieve primary goals. Some see significant results in 12 weeks; others benefit from 6-12 months of support for more complex goals or chronic condition management. After the intensive phase, many clients continue with monthly or quarterly maintenance sessions. There's no rigid timeline—coaching duration is flexible based on your needs and progress.

What's the difference between wellness coaching and therapy/counseling?

Wellness coaches focus on present and future—setting goals, building skills, implementing strategies, and creating accountability for health behavior change. Coaches don't diagnose or treat mental health conditions. Therapists/counselors focus on mental health treatment, past trauma, emotional processing, and clinical conditions like depression or anxiety. Many people benefit from BOTH—coaching for health behaviors and therapy for mental health. We can coordinate with your therapist if you're receiving both services.

Will insurance cover wellness coaching?

Coverage varies significantly. Some insurance plans cover wellness coaching or "health coaching" as a preventive service, especially when related to chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension, obesity). However, many plans do not cover coaching. We recommend checking with your insurance provider about coverage for health coaching or lifestyle medicine services. We can provide documentation and codes to support reimbursement claims. Self-pay options are available.

What happens if I miss sessions or fall off track?

Life happens—we understand! Missing sessions or having setbacks doesn't mean failure; it's part of the process. Your coach will help you get back on track without judgment, identify what caused the lapse, problem-solve obstacles, and adjust strategies as needed. Coaching teaches you how to recover from setbacks rather than letting them derail all progress. The key is re-engaging rather than quitting entirely.

Do I need to track my food/activity/habits?

Tracking depends on your goals and what works for your personality. Many clients benefit from tracking initially (food logs, step counts, sleep hours, weight) to establish awareness and identify patterns. Tracking provides objective data for your coach to review. However, if tracking feels overwhelming or triggering, we'll find alternative monitoring methods. The goal is accountability—not obsessive tracking. We tailor monitoring strategies to your preferences.

Can coaching help if I've failed at diets/programs before?

Absolutely—that's exactly why coaching is effective! Previous "failures" usually aren't about willpower or lack of discipline. They're about unsustainable approaches, unaddressed barriers (stress, emotional eating, poor sleep), lack of support, or plans that didn't fit your life. Coaching identifies WHY past attempts failed, addresses root causes, provides accountability, and creates realistic strategies you can actually maintain. Our approach is completely different from restrictive diet programs.

What credentials do your wellness coaches have?

Our wellness coaches hold professional certifications from recognized organizations such as the National Board for Health & Wellness Coaching (NBC-HWC), American Council on Exercise (ACE), National Academy of Sports Medicine (NASM), or similar credentialing bodies. They're trained in behavior change psychology, motivational interviewing, lifestyle medicine, health promotion, and chronic disease management. They work collaboratively with our medical team for integrated care.

Can my family or partner be involved in coaching?

Yes! Family involvement can be extremely beneficial, especially when household members affect your environment, meal planning, or motivation. We can include your partner or family members in select sessions, provide family-focused strategies, or offer couple's wellness coaching. Having a supportive home environment dramatically improves success rates. We'll discuss how family involvement fits your situation.

How is wellness coaching different from working with a personal trainer?

Personal trainers focus primarily on exercise—designing workouts, teaching proper form, and providing accountability for fitness. Wellness coaches take a holistic approach addressing nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress management, habit change, and mindset across all areas of health. If your primary goal is fitness programming, a trainer is ideal. If you need comprehensive lifestyle change support beyond just exercise, wellness coaching is more appropriate. Some clients work with both!
