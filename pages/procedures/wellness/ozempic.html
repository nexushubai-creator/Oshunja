Overview

Ozempic (semaglutide) is a once-weekly injectable medication that has transformed weight management for patients in Kingston, Jamaica. Originally developed for type 2 diabetes, this GLP-1 receptor agonist works by mimicking the body's natural hunger-regulating hormones, reducing appetite and promoting significant, sustainable weight loss when combined with lifestyle modifications.

At OSHUNJA, we offer medically supervised Ozempic treatment as part of a comprehensive weight management program. Our Kingston-based team provides thorough evaluation, personalized dosing, nutritional guidance, and ongoing monitoring to maximize your results while minimizing side effects. Unlike quick-fix diets, Ozempic addresses the biological mechanisms that make weight loss difficult, helping you achieve long-term success.

Clinical studies demonstrate that patients typically lose 12-15% of their body weight over 68 weeks. Beyond weight loss, many patients experience improved energy, better blood sugar control, reduced cardiovascular risk, and enhanced confidence. Our holistic approach ensures you receive the support, education, and medical oversight necessary for a safe and successful transformation journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight can I expect to lose on Ozempic?

Clinical studies show patients lose an average of 12-15% of their starting body weight over 68 weeks when combined with lifestyle changes. Individual results vary based on starting weight, adherence to treatment, diet, and exercise habits.

What are the common side effects?

Most common side effects are gastrointestinal: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation. These typically occur when starting treatment or increasing dose, and usually decrease over time. Our Kingston team provides strategies to minimize discomfort.

Do I have to inject myself?

Yes, Ozempic is self-administered via weekly subcutaneous injection. However, the pen device is pre-filled and very easy to use. We provide thorough training at our Kingston clinic, and most patients become comfortable with injections after the first few weeks.

How long do I need to stay on Ozempic?

Ozempic is typically a long-term treatment. Many patients regain weight when stopping the medication, so continued use is often recommended to maintain results. We'll work with you to develop a sustainable plan that fits your goals and budget.

Is Ozempic covered by insurance in Jamaica?

Coverage varies by insurance provider. Ozempic is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes (more likely covered) but often prescribed off-label for weight loss (less likely covered). Our Kingston team can provide documentation to support insurance claims when applicable.

Can I drink alcohol while on Ozempic?

Moderate alcohol consumption is generally acceptable, but alcohol may increase risk of low blood sugar and worsen gastrointestinal side effects. We recommend limiting alcohol, especially when first starting treatment, and discussing your specific situation with our medical team.

What's the difference between Ozempic and Mounjaro?

Ozempic is a GLP-1 agonist, while Mounjaro is a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist. Mounjaro often produces slightly more weight loss but may have more side effects. Both are excellent options, and we'll help determine which is best for you during your Kingston consultation.

Will I need to follow a special diet?

There's no specific "Ozempic diet," but we recommend focusing on protein-rich, nutrient-dense foods with plenty of vegetables and adequate hydration. Smaller, more frequent meals may help manage side effects. Our dietitian provides personalized guidance for optimal results.

Can men use Ozempic for weight loss?

Absolutely! Ozempic is effective for both men and women struggling with weight management. The medication works the same regardless of gender, and we treat many male patients at our Kingston clinic.

What happens if I miss a dose?

If you miss a dose and it's within 5 days of when you should have taken it, inject as soon as you remember. If more than 5 days have passed, skip that dose and resume your regular schedule. Never take two doses within 48 hours of each other.
