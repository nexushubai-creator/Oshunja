Overview

Vulvar and Vaginal Cyst and Wart Removal at OSHUNJA provides expert treatment for benign cysts (Bartholin cysts, inclusion cysts, sebaceous cysts) and genital warts affecting the intimate area. Our Kingston-based gynecological specialists diagnose and remove these growths using minimally invasive techniques, providing immediate relief from discomfort, preventing complications, and restoring confidence. We offer completely confidential care for these common but embarrassing conditions with maximum discretion and surgical expertise.

Vulvar and vaginal cysts are fluid-filled sacs that can cause swelling, pain, discomfort during sitting or intimacy, and aesthetic concerns. Bartholin cysts form when glands at the vaginal opening become blocked. Inclusion cysts develop from trapped skin cells. Genital warts are caused by HPV and appear as small flesh-colored bumps. Treatment involves surgical drainage, excision, or destruction depending on the type. Most procedures are minimally invasive with quick recovery and excellent results.

Our board-certified surgeons understand the sensitive nature of these conditions and provide judgment-free, compassionate care. We ensure complete privacy during consultations and procedures, accurate diagnosis through examination, personalized treatment plans, and minimal scarring with advanced techniques. Your comfort and discretion are our highest priorities at our private Kingston facility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the cyst/wart come back after removal?

Bartholin cysts can recur (20-30% rate) if the gland continues producing fluid. Marsupialization reduces recurrence risk. Genital warts may recur due to persistent HPV infection, even after complete removal.

Is cyst/wart removal painful?

Procedures are performed under local anesthesia so you won't feel pain during removal. Post-procedure discomfort is manageable with prescribed pain medication and typically resolves within days.

How long does the procedure take?

Most cyst drainages or small wart removals take 15-30 minutes. More complex procedures like marsupialization or multiple wart removals may take 30-60 minutes.

Can I have this done while pregnant?

Simple cyst drainage can be performed during pregnancy if needed. Wart removal is generally postponed until after delivery unless causing significant problems.

Do I need to tell my partner about genital warts?

Yes. Genital warts are caused by HPV which is sexually transmitted. Partners should be informed so they can be examined and receive HPV vaccination if appropriate.

How much does removal cost in Jamaica?

Pricing depends on procedure complexity and number of lesions. We provide detailed cost estimates during consultation including insurance coverage information.

Will there be scarring?

With proper technique, scarring is minimal and hidden in natural tissue folds. Most scars fade significantly over time and are not readily visible.

When can I resume sexual activity?

Most patients can resume intimacy after 2-4 weeks once healing is complete. We provide specific guidance based on your procedure type and healing progress.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Yes. Our surgeons have extensive experience with all skin tones including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. Healing is the same regardless of complexion.
