Overview

Clitoral Hood Reduction (hoodectomy) at OSHUNJA surgically removes excess prepuce tissue covering the clitoris to enhance comfort, improve aesthetics, and potentially increase sensitivity. Our Kingston-based cosmetic gynecology specialists perform this delicate procedure to address excess clitoral hood tissue that causes discomfort during activities, reduces sensitivity, or creates aesthetic concerns. The surgery provides natural-looking results that improve both function and appearance.

The clitoral hood (prepuce) naturally protects the clitoris, but some women have excess tissue that folds over or extends beyond the clitoral area. This can cause discomfort during exercise or intimacy, reduce sensitivity by limiting direct stimulation, or create aesthetic concerns. Clitoral hood reduction carefully removes excess tissue while preserving protective function, creating improved exposure and enhanced comfort. The procedure is often combined with labiaplasty for comprehensive intimate rejuvenation.

Our board-certified surgeon brings specialized training in intimate aesthetics, combining surgical precision with an artistic understanding of female anatomy. We provide completely confidential care in a private, luxurious environment where you can discuss your concerns openly without judgment. Your comfort and satisfaction are our highest priorities throughout your journey at our discrete Kingston facility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will clitoral hood reduction increase sensitivity?

Many patients report enhanced sensitivity due to improved clitoral exposure, but individual results vary. The procedure removes tissue that may be limiting direct stimulation, potentially improving intimate sensation.

How is this different from labiaplasty?

Labiaplasty reduces the labia minora (inner lips), while clitoral hood reduction addresses excess prepuce tissue covering the clitoris. They're often performed together for comprehensive intimate rejuvenation but address different anatomical areas.

Is the procedure painful?

Performed under anesthesia, you won't feel pain during surgery. Post-operative discomfort is manageable with prescribed medication and typically subsides within a few days to a week.

When can I resume intimate activities?

Most patients are cleared for sexual activity after 4-6 weeks, once healing is complete. We provide specific guidance based on your individual healing progress at follow-up appointments.

Will there be visible scarring?

Scarring is typically minimal and hidden within natural tissue folds. Our precise surgical technique and proper wound care instructions minimize scar visibility. Scars continue to fade over time.

How much tissue will be removed?

The amount varies based on your anatomy and goals. We carefully plan the reduction during consultation to remove excess tissue while maintaining protective function and natural appearance.

Can this affect my ability to orgasm?

Properly performed hoodectomy should not negatively affect orgasm. Some patients report enhanced sensation due to improved clitoral exposure. We preserve nerve function and protective tissue during surgery.

How much does clitoral hood reduction cost in Jamaica?

Pricing depends on procedure complexity and whether combining with other treatments. We provide detailed cost estimates during your consultation, including financing options and package pricing.

Is this treatment confidential?

Absolutely. We maintain complete discretion and confidentiality for all intimate procedures. Your privacy is our highest priority from consultation through recovery.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Yes. Our surgeons have extensive experience with all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. Healing is the same regardless of complexion with proper surgical technique.
