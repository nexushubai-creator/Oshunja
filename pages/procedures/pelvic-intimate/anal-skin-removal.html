Overview

Anal Skin Tag Removal at OSHUNJA surgically removes benign perianal skin tags that cause discomfort, hygiene challenges, or self-consciousness. Our Kingston-based specialists perform this minor outpatient procedure to eliminate excess skin around the anal area resulting from childbirth trauma, chronic constipation, hemorrhoids, or aging. The procedure provides immediate relief from irritation and improved confidence through complete discretion and expert surgical care.

Anal skin tags are small, soft growths of excess skin around the anus that can cause itching, difficulty with hygiene, bleeding from irritation, and discomfort during sitting or intimacy. While harmless, they can be bothersome and embarrassing. Surgical removal is quick, effective, and provides permanent results. The procedure excises the skin tags at their base, allowing for complete healing with minimal scarring and maximum comfort improvement.

Our board-certified surgeon understands the sensitive and private nature of this concern. We provide completely confidential care in a comfortable, judgment-free environment where your dignity is respected. Your consultation is conducted with maximum discretion, and we ensure you feel comfortable discussing your concerns throughout your care at our private Kingston facility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is anal skin tag removal painful?

The procedure is performed under anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during removal. Post-operative discomfort is manageable with prescribed pain medication and typically subsides within a few days to a week.

Will the skin tags come back after removal?

Once removed, specific skin tags don't regrow. However, new skin tags can develop in different areas if underlying causes (constipation, straining, hemorrhoids) aren't managed. We provide guidance on prevention.

How long does recovery take?

Most patients return to normal activities within 1-2 weeks. Complete healing typically occurs within 2-3 weeks with proper care and hygiene.

Will there be visible scarring?

With proper technique, scarring is minimal and hidden in the natural folds of the perianal area. Scars typically fade significantly over time and are not readily visible.

Can I prevent new skin tags from forming?

Managing constipation, avoiding straining during bowel movements, treating hemorrhoids promptly, and maintaining healthy weight can help prevent new skin tag formation.

How soon after childbirth can I have this done?

We typically recommend waiting at least 6 months postpartum to allow complete healing. If you're breastfeeding, we'll discuss timing based on your individual situation.

Is this treatment confidential?

Absolutely. We maintain complete discretion and confidentiality for all procedures. Your privacy is our highest priority from consultation through recovery.

How much does anal skin tag removal cost in Jamaica?

Pricing depends on the number and size of skin tags being removed. We provide detailed cost estimates during your consultation, including financing options if needed.

When can I return to work?

Most patients return to desk work within 2-3 days. Jobs requiring heavy lifting or prolonged standing may require 1-2 weeks off. We provide specific guidance based on your occupation.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Yes. Our surgeons have extensive experience with all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. Healing is the same regardless of complexion with proper surgical technique.
