Overview

Perineoplasty at OSHUNJA surgically repairs and reconstructs the perineum (the area between the vagina and anus) to restore structure, function, and appearance after childbirth trauma or injury. Our Kingston-based cosmetic gynecology specialists perform this reconstructive procedure to address perineal tears, scarring, or laxity from vaginal delivery, helping women reclaim comfort, confidence, and intimate wellness through expert surgical restoration.

This procedure addresses the perineal body that can be damaged during childbirth, particularly with episiotomies or severe tearing. Perineoplasty repairs torn or stretched perineal muscles, removes excess scar tissue, and reconstructs the area to restore anatomical support. The result is improved structural integrity, reduced discomfort during intimacy, and enhanced vaginal opening aesthetics and tightness.

Our board-certified surgeon brings extensive experience in perineal reconstruction, combining surgical precision with an understanding of postpartum concerns and intimate wellness. We provide a confidential, compassionate environment where you can openly discuss your concerns, ensuring complete privacy and expert care throughout your journey at our discrete Kingston facility.

How It Works & What To Expect

Confidential discussion of your perineal concerns and goals. Examination to assess perineal damage, scarring, or laxity. We explain the surgical approach and create your personalized reconstruction plan.

Scheduled follow-ups to monitor healing and ensure satisfaction. Clearance for resuming intimate activity provided once healing is complete.

Recovery & Aftercare

First Week

  • Rest and avoid strenuous activities
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling and discomfort
  • Take prescribed pain medication and stool softeners
  • Maintain strict pelvic rest‚Äîno intercourse, tampons, or douching
  • Follow specific hygiene instructions to prevent infection

2-3 Weeks

  • Gradually resume light activities as comfort allows
  • Most swelling and discomfort should subside
  • Continue pelvic rest‚Äîno sexual activity
  • Avoid heavy lifting, exercise, and prolonged sitting
  • Follow-up appointment to monitor healing

4-6 Weeks

  • Resume most normal activities including light exercise
  • Healing progressing well with reduced discomfort
  • Continue avoiding sexual activity until cleared
  • Dissolvable sutures should be absorbed
  • Schedule clearance examination

6-8 Weeks & Beyond

  • Cleared for sexual activity once healing confirmed
  • Resume all normal activities including exercise
  • Final results become apparent as healing completes
  • Schedule final follow-up to assess satisfaction

Aftercare Instructions

Hygiene: Gentle cleansing after bowel movements, front to back
Pelvic Rest: Strict abstinence from intercourse for 6-8 weeks
Activities: Avoid exercise, heavy lifting for 4-6 weeks
Bowel Care: Use stool softeners to prevent straining
Comfort: Use ice packs and prescribed pain management
Follow-up: Attend all appointments for healing assessment
When to Contact Us: Call immediately if you experience severe pain, heavy bleeding, signs of infection (fever, foul discharge), difficulty with bowel movements, or any concerning symptoms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does perineoplasty recovery take?

Initial recovery takes 2-3 weeks. Full recovery with clearance for sexual activity typically takes 6-8 weeks. Complete healing and final results are achieved at 3-6 months.

Will it help with pain during intercourse?

Yes. Perineoplasty specifically addresses painful scar tissue and repairs damaged perineal tissue that causes discomfort during intimacy. Most patients experience significant pain reduction or elimination.

Can I have future pregnancies after perineoplasty?

We strongly recommend completing childbearing before perineoplasty as vaginal delivery can damage the reconstruction. If you do become pregnant, C-section delivery can preserve results.

Is the procedure painful?

Performed under anesthesia, you won't feel pain during surgery. Post-operative discomfort is manageable with prescribed medication and typically subsides within 1-2 weeks.

Will it affect bowel movements?

With proper surgical technique, bowel function is preserved. We provide stool softeners to prevent straining during initial healing. Normal bowel function resumes as healing progresses.

How is perineoplasty different from vaginoplasty?

Perineoplasty repairs the perineal body (between vagina and anus), while vaginoplasty tightens the vaginal canal. They're often combined for comprehensive reconstruction but address different areas.

How much does perineoplasty cost in Jamaica?

Pricing depends on extent of reconstruction needed and whether combining with other procedures. We provide detailed cost estimates during consultation, including financing options and package pricing.

Can it fix old episiotomy scars?

Yes. Perineoplasty specifically removes and revises old episiotomy scars, replacing them with properly healed tissue that doesn't cause pain or discomfort.

Is this treatment confidential?

Absolutely. We maintain complete discretion and confidentiality for all intimate procedures. Your privacy is our highest priority from consultation through recovery.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Yes. Our surgeons have extensive experience treating patients of all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. Healing is the same regardless of skin tone with proper technique.
