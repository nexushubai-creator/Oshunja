Overview

Lichen Sclerosus Surgical Treatment at OSHUNJA provides reconstructive surgery for advanced cases where medical management hasn't prevented anatomical changes. Our Kingston-based gynecological surgeons perform vulvar reconstruction to release tissue fusion, remove severely affected tissue, and restore normal anatomy in patients with progressive lichen sclerosus scarring. This surgical intervention addresses labial fusion, clitoral adhesions, narrowing of the vaginal opening, and severe architectural distortion that affects urination, sexual function, and quality of life.

While most lichen sclerosus patients achieve excellent control with topical medications, some develop irreversible scarring despite treatment. Surgical intervention becomes necessary when adhesions prevent normal function, fusion obscures anatomy, or severe scarring causes persistent pain. Procedures include adhesiolysis (separating fused tissues), excision of heavily scarred areas, and reconstructive techniques to restore vulvar contours. Surgery doesn't cure the underlying condition but dramatically improves function and appearance when medical therapy alone is insufficient.

Our board-certified surgeons specialize in delicate vulvar reconstruction with experience managing this challenging condition. We provide comprehensive care including pre-operative optimization of medical therapy, expert surgical technique minimizing recurrence risk, and post-operative protocols preventing re-adhesion. Your consultation includes thorough examination, discussion of surgical goals and realistic expectations, and coordination with ongoing medical management at our discrete Kingston facility.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Comprehensive Evaluation

Thorough assessment of anatomical changes, functional limitations, and treatment history. We determine extent of reconstruction needed and optimal timing relative to medical therapy.

2

Pre-Operative Optimization

Maximizing medical therapy before surgery to control active inflammation. Pre-surgical counseling about realistic expectations, potential complications, and post-op care requirements.

3

Surgical Reconstruction

Release of adhesions and fusion using precise dissection. Excision of severely scarred tissue when necessary. Reconstruction of normal vulvar architecture with attention to preserving function.

4

Post-Operative Protocols

Aggressive medical therapy to prevent recurrence. Dilator protocols if needed to maintain opening. Close monitoring for signs of re-adhesion or inflammation.

5

Long-Term Management

Continued medical therapy to control underlying disease. Regular follow-ups to monitor for recurrence. Intervention if early adhesions detected.

  • Excisional surgery: Removing severely affected tissue
  • Vulvar reconstruction: Restoring normal anatomy and contours

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Surgical Treatment May Be Right for You If:

  • You have labial fusion despite medical treatment—adhesiolysis restores separation
  • You cannot urinate normally due to scarring—reconstruction opens urethral area
  • You're unable to have intercourse due to vaginal opening narrowing—surgery widens entrance
  • You have clitoral adhesions causing pain or loss of sensation—release improves function
  • You've maximized medical therapy but still have severe architectural distortion
  • You're committed to post-operative medical therapy preventing recurrence
  • You understand surgery doesn't cure the condition but addresses complications

Important Surgical Considerations:

  • Active Disease Control: Inflammation should be minimized before surgery—optimal timing critical
  • Recurrence Risk: Adhesions can reform without aggressive post-op medical therapy
  • Dilator Use: May require vaginal dilators post-surgery to maintain opening
  • Realistic Expectations: Surgery improves function but doesn't provide perfect anatomy or cure disease
  • Lifelong Management: Continued topical therapy essential after surgery
  • Potential Complications: Bleeding, infection, scarring, recurrence possible
Not Recommended If: Active severe inflammation present, unable to comply with post-op medical therapy, unrealistic expectations about cure, or insufficient functional impairment to justify surgical risks.

Recovery & Aftercare

First Week

  • Rest and avoid strenuous activities
  • Apply prescribed medications to surgical sites
  • Take pain medication as needed
  • Strict pelvic rest—no intercourse or tampons
  • Gentle cleansing with specific instructions

2-4 Weeks

  • Continue topical medications to prevent recurrence
  • Gradually resume light activities
  • Begin dilator use if prescribed
  • Follow-up to assess healing and check for adhesions
  • Continue pelvic rest until cleared

6-8 Weeks

  • Most patients cleared for sexual activity
  • Resume all normal activities
  • Continue medical therapy as prescribed
  • Regular dilator use if recommended
  • Monitor for early signs of re-adhesion

Long-Term Management

  • Lifelong topical therapy to prevent recurrence
  • Regular monitoring for adhesion reformation
  • Early intervention if problems detected
  • Annual cancer surveillance continues

Critical Aftercare Instructions

Frequently Asked Questions

Will surgery cure my lichen sclerosus?

No. Surgery addresses complications like fusion and scarring but doesn't cure the underlying condition. Lifelong medical therapy remains essential to prevent recurrence and control inflammation.

Will the adhesions come back?

There's a risk of recurrence without aggressive post-operative medical therapy. Consistent use of prescribed medications and regular monitoring minimize this risk significantly.

How long until I can have sex after surgery?

Most patients are cleared for intimacy after 6-8 weeks once healing is complete. Dilator use may be recommended before resuming intercourse to maintain the vaginal opening.

Is the surgery painful?

Surgery is performed under anesthesia. Post-operative discomfort is manageable with prescribed pain medication and typically improves significantly within 1-2 weeks.

What are the risks of surgery?

Risks include bleeding, infection, scarring, recurrence of adhesions, and unsatisfactory results. Our expert surgical technique and comprehensive post-op protocols minimize these risks.

Do I need to use dilators after surgery?

Many patients require vaginal dilators post-operatively to prevent the vaginal opening from narrowing again. Your surgeon will provide specific guidance based on your procedure.

How much does this surgery cost in Jamaica?

Pricing depends on the extent of reconstruction needed. We provide detailed cost estimates during consultation including insurance coverage information for this medically necessary procedure.

Can I have this done if I'm still having children?

Surgery can be performed regardless of childbearing status, though pregnancy and delivery don't typically worsen lichen sclerosus. We'll discuss timing based on your individual situation.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Yes. Our surgeons have extensive experience with all skin tones. Healing is the same regardless of complexion with proper surgical technique and post-operative care.
