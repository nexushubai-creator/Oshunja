Overview

Labiaplasty is a surgical procedure designed to reduce and reshape the labia minora (inner vaginal lips) for improved comfort, symmetry, and aesthetic appearance. At OSHUNJA, our fellowship-trained gynecologic surgeons provide compassionate, expert care in a private, judgment-free environment.

Many women experience discomfort during physical activities, exercise, or intimacy due to enlarged or asymmetric labia. This procedure addresses both functional concerns and aesthetic preferences, helping you feel more comfortable in your body. Our surgeons use advanced techniques to preserve natural sensation while achieving beautiful, natural-looking results.

Located in Kingston, Jamaica, OSHUNJA offers world-class cosmetic gynecology with the discretion and personalized attention you deserve. Each consultation is private and tailored to your unique anatomy and goals.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

You May Be an Ideal Candidate If:

  • You experience discomfort during exercise or intimacy
  • You feel self-conscious about the appearance of your labia
  • You have asymmetry or elongation of the labia minora
  • You're in good overall health
  • You have realistic expectations about results
  • You're not currently pregnant or breastfeeding
  • You're a non-smoker or willing to quit before surgery

Important Considerations:

  • Age: Best performed after full physical maturity (18+)
  • Pregnancy: Procedure should be postponed; wait 6+ months after delivery
  • Medical Conditions: Some conditions may require additional precautions
  • Medications: Blood thinners must be stopped before surgery
  • Scarring: History of keloid formation should be discussed
  • Future Childbirth: Can be performed before having children
Contraindications: This procedure is not recommended for those with active infections, uncontrolled diabetes, or certain blood clotting disorders. A thorough medical evaluation will determine your candidacy.

Recovery & Aftercare

First 24-48 Hours

  • Rest with ice packs to reduce swelling
  • Take prescribed pain medication as directed
  • Keep the area clean and dry
  • Avoid standing or sitting for prolonged periods
  • No physical activity or lifting

First Week

  • Gentle cleaning with provided solution
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing
  • Light walking is encouraged
  • No sexual activity or tampons
  • Follow-up appointment to check healing

2-6 Weeks

  • Gradually resume normal activities
  • Swelling continues to decrease
  • May return to light exercise after 4 weeks
  • Sexual activity after 6 weeks or with surgeon approval

3-6 Months

  • Complete healing achieved
  • Scars mature and fade
  • Final results fully visible
  • All activities can be resumed

Aftercare Instructions

Hygiene: Gently cleanse with lukewarm water after using the bathroom
Clothing: Wear loose, breathable cotton underwear and clothing
Activity: Avoid strenuous exercise, cycling, and horseback riding for 6 weeks
Diet: Stay hydrated and eat high-fiber foods to prevent constipation
Medication: Take antibiotics as prescribed to prevent infection
Follow-up: Attend all scheduled check-ups to monitor healing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is labiaplasty painful?

The procedure itself is performed under anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during surgery. Afterward, most patients describe mild to moderate discomfort similar to menstrual cramps, which is easily managed with prescribed pain medication. Most patients rate their pain as 3-4 out of 10 during the first few days.

Will I have visible scars?

Our surgeons use advanced techniques to minimize scarring, placing incisions in natural folds. Over time, scars typically fade to thin, barely visible lines. Most patients report that scars are virtually undetectable after 6-12 months.

How long before I can return to work?

Most patients return to desk work within 5-7 days. If your job involves physical labor or prolonged standing, you may need 2-3 weeks. We provide work notes as needed.

When can I resume sexual activity?

We recommend waiting 6 weeks before resuming sexual activity to allow complete healing. Some patients may need longer depending on their healing progress. Your surgeon will advise you at your follow-up appointments.

Will this affect sensitivity or sexual pleasure?

When performed by an experienced surgeon, labiaplasty should not negatively affect sensation. In fact, many patients report enhanced comfort and pleasure during intimacy. Our technique preserves nerve pathways and blood supply to maintain natural sensation.

Can I have children after labiaplasty?

Yes, labiaplasty does not affect your ability to become pregnant or deliver a baby. However, we generally recommend waiting until you've completed your family, as vaginal delivery may impact results.

Is labiaplasty covered by insurance?

Most insurance companies consider labiaplasty cosmetic and don't provide coverage. However, if the procedure is medically necessary due to chronic irritation or pain, partial coverage may be possible. We can provide documentation to support insurance claims when applicable.

How much does labiaplasty cost in Jamaica?

The cost varies depending on the extent of the procedure and whether it's combined with other treatments. During your consultation, we'll provide a detailed quote. We offer flexible payment plans to make treatment accessible.

What's the difference between labiaplasty and vaginoplasty?

Labiaplasty reduces the external labia minora (inner lips), while vaginoplasty tightens the internal vaginal canal. They address different concerns and can be performed separately or together.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Absolutely. Our surgeons have extensive experience treating patients of all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. We use techniques specifically tailored to minimize pigmentation changes and optimize healing for darker skin types.
