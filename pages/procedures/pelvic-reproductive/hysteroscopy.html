Overview

Hysteroscopy at OSHUNJA provides minimally invasive visualization and treatment of uterine conditions through a thin telescope inserted through your cervix. This gold-standard diagnostic procedure allows our Kingston gynecologists to directly examine your uterine cavity, identify abnormalities, and often treat problems during the same visit without incisions or lengthy recovery.

Whether investigating abnormal bleeding, recurrent miscarriages, infertility, or removing polyps and fibroids, hysteroscopy offers real-time visual diagnosis superior to ultrasound or blind procedures. Our state-of-the-art hysteroscopy suite features high-definition cameras and specialized instruments enabling precise treatment while preserving your fertility and minimizing discomfort.

As one of the few facilities in Jamaica offering both diagnostic and operative hysteroscopy, we provide comprehensive care from initial evaluation to definitive treatment. Most procedures are performed in-office with minimal anesthesia, allowing you to return home within hours with answers and solutions to your gynecological concerns.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Pre-Procedure Preparation

We review your medical history, symptoms, and imaging results. Schedule during non-menstruating phase for optimal visualization. You may receive medication to soften your cervix beforehand.

2

Anesthesia & Positioning

Local anesthesia, conscious sedation, or general anesthesia administered based on procedure complexity. You're positioned similarly to a pelvic exam for cervical access.

3

Hysteroscope Insertion

A thin telescope with camera and light is gently inserted through your cervix. Sterile fluid expands your uterine cavity for clear visualization. The hysteroscope never penetrates uterine tissue‚Äîit simply provides a window into your uterus.

4

Visual Examination & Treatment

High-definition camera displays your uterine cavity on a monitor. We identify polyps, fibroids, adhesions, or abnormal tissue. If needed, specialized instruments remove polyps, take biopsies, or treat abnormalities during the same visit.

5

Recovery & Results

Procedure completes in 15-45 minutes depending on complexity. Rest briefly in recovery, then go home same day. Results discussed immediately; biopsy findings available within 7-10 days if tissue samples were taken.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Hysteroscopy Is Recommended If:

  • You experience abnormal uterine bleeding, heavy periods, or bleeding between cycles requiring investigation beyond ultrasound
  • Imaging revealed suspected uterine polyps or submucosal fibroids affecting your symptoms or fertility
  • You've had recurrent miscarriages and need evaluation for uterine abnormalities like septum or adhesions
  • Unexplained infertility persists despite normal testing‚Äîhysteroscopy identifies subtle uterine issues
  • Your IUD is embedded, displaced, or cannot be removed with standard techniques
  • You need endometrial biopsy with direct visualization to improve diagnostic accuracy
  • Previous cesarean delivery or D&C raised concern for Asherman's syndrome (uterine scarring)

Important Considerations:

  • Diagnostic vs. Operative: Simple diagnostic hysteroscopy uses local anesthesia; operative procedures removing polyps/fibroids may require general anesthesia
  • Timing: Schedule after menstruation ends but before ovulation for best visualization‚Äîtypically days 5-12 of cycle
  • Pregnancy: Cannot perform hysteroscopy if you're pregnant‚Äîpregnancy test done before procedure
  • Active Infection: Pelvic infections must be treated before hysteroscopy to prevent spread
  • Cervical Issues: Severe cervical stenosis may make hysteroscopy difficult but can usually be managed
  • Follow-Up: Results often immediate, but biopsy findings take 7-10 days if tissue samples obtained
Note: Hysteroscopy provides diagnosis and treatment ultrasound cannot match‚Äîit's the gold standard for evaluating uterine cavity abnormalities.

Days 2-3

  • Gradually resume light activities and work
  • Cramping diminishes significantly

Weeks 2-4

  • Resume fertility treatments if applicable

Frequently Asked Questions

Does hysteroscopy hurt?

Discomfort varies by individual. Diagnostic hysteroscopy with local anesthesia causes mild cramping similar to a Pap smear. Operative hysteroscopy is performed under conscious sedation or general anesthesia, so you feel nothing during the procedure. Post-procedure cramping is manageable with over-the-counter pain relievers.

How long does hysteroscopy take?

Simple diagnostic hysteroscopy takes 5-15 minutes. Operative hysteroscopy removing polyps or fibroids takes 15-45 minutes depending on complexity. Including preparation and recovery, expect to be at our facility for 1-3 hours total.

Can I get pregnant after hysteroscopy?

Yes. Hysteroscopy often improves fertility by removing polyps, fibroids, or scar tissue that prevent pregnancy. Most doctors recommend waiting one menstrual cycle after hysteroscopy before trying to conceive, allowing your uterine lining to heal completely.

What will the doctor see during hysteroscopy?

The camera displays your uterine cavity, endometrial lining, tubal openings, and cervical canal. We look for polyps, fibroids, adhesions, septum, abnormal tissue growth, inflammation, and other structural abnormalities invisible on ultrasound.

Is hysteroscopy better than ultrasound?

Ultrasound is excellent for initial screening but hysteroscopy provides direct visualization impossible with imaging. Hysteroscopy detects small polyps, adhesions, and subtle abnormalities ultrasound misses. It also allows immediate treatment‚Äîultrasound only diagnoses.

When can I return to work after hysteroscopy?

Most women return to work 1-2 days after diagnostic hysteroscopy. Operative hysteroscopy may require 2-3 days off depending on what was done. Desk jobs can often resume next day; physically demanding work may need a few extra days.

Will I need to stay overnight in the hospital?

No. Hysteroscopy is an outpatient procedure. After brief recovery (30 minutes to 2 hours), you go home the same day. Arrange for someone to drive you if you received sedation.

What are the risks of hysteroscopy?

Hysteroscopy is very safe. Rare complications include uterine perforation (1%), infection (<1%), and excessive bleeding (<1%). Fluid overload can occur with lengthy operative procedures but is monitored carefully. Serious complications are extremely rare with experienced surgeons.

Can fibroids be removed during hysteroscopy?

Submucosal fibroids (those protruding into the uterine cavity) can be removed hysteroscopically. Intramural and subserosal fibroids require different surgical approaches like laparoscopic or abdominal myomectomy. We'll determine your fibroid type during evaluation.

Is hysteroscopy covered by insurance?

When medically necessary for symptoms like abnormal bleeding, recurrent miscarriage, or fertility issues, hysteroscopy is typically covered by insurance. Diagnostic and operative hysteroscopy both qualify. Verify your specific coverage beforehand‚Äîour team assists with insurance questions.
