Overview

Your Annual Well-Woman Exam at OSHUNJA provides comprehensive preventive gynecological care essential for maintaining reproductive health and detecting potential issues early. This yearly visit combines screening tests, physical examination, and personalized health counseling tailored to your age, medical history, and individual risk factors‚Äîall in our comfortable Kingston facility.

Regular annual exams catch cervical cancer, sexually transmitted infections, and other gynecological conditions in their earliest, most treatable stages. Beyond screenings, your visit addresses birth control needs, sexual health concerns, menstrual irregularities, and menopause management. We provide evidence-based guidance on topics you might hesitate to discuss elsewhere, ensuring you have the information needed to make informed decisions about your body.

Our Kingston gynecologists create a judgment-free environment where your questions and concerns receive thorough, compassionate attention. Whether you're 18 or 80, sexually active or not, your annual exam is the cornerstone of proactive women's health care‚Äîand we're here to make it as comfortable and informative as possible.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Medical History Review

Your provider reviews your health history, medications, menstrual patterns, sexual activity, contraception use, and any concerns you may have. This comprehensive discussion ensures personalized care.

2

Physical Examination

Comprehensive breast and pelvic examination to assess your overall reproductive health. We check for abnormalities, evaluate tissue health, and ensure everything appears normal.

3

Pap Smear & Cervical Screening

Cervical cell sample collected for cancer screening as recommended by current guidelines. This painless test detects precancerous changes early when most treatable.

4

Additional Testing & Counseling

STI screening, blood pressure measurement, and other age-appropriate health assessments. We discuss contraception, sexual health, and any symptoms or concerns you're experiencing.

5

Results Review & Follow-Up Planning

Test results typically available within 7-10 days. We contact you with findings and recommendations, scheduling any necessary follow-up appointments or treatments.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

You Should Schedule an Annual Exam If:

  • You're 21 years or older and haven't had a gynecological exam in the past year‚Äîregular preventive care is essential for early detection
  • You're sexually active and need STI screening, contraception counseling, or sexual health guidance
  • You're planning pregnancy and want to ensure optimal reproductive health before conceiving
  • You experience irregular periods, pelvic pain, abnormal bleeding, or unusual vaginal discharge that needs evaluation
  • You're approaching or experiencing menopause and need guidance managing symptoms and hormone changes
  • You have a family history of gynecological cancers and require increased surveillance and risk assessment
  • You simply want peace of mind that your reproductive health is optimal and nothing requires attention

Important Screening Guidelines:

  • Pap Smear Frequency: Every 3 years for ages 21-65, or every 5 years when combined with HPV testing (discuss your schedule with your provider)
  • HPV Vaccination: If you haven't received HPV vaccine and are under 45, discuss vaccination to prevent cervical cancer
  • STI Testing: Annual screening recommended for sexually active women under 25, or those with new/multiple partners regardless of age
  • Breast Examination: Clinical breast exam during annual visit, with mammography starting at age 40 (earlier if family history)
  • Best Timing: Schedule when you're not menstruating for most comfortable pelvic exam and accurate Pap smear results
  • Open Communication: This is your opportunity to discuss sensitive topics in a judgment-free environment‚Äîno question is off-limits
When to Seek Care Between Annual Exams: Contact us immediately if you experience severe pelvic pain, abnormal bleeding, unusual discharge with odor, or any gynecological symptoms causing concern.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I need an annual exam?

Despite the name, "annual" exams may not always be yearly for everyone. Pap smears are typically every 3 years for women ages 21-65, or every 5 years when combined with HPV testing. However, physical exams, STI screening, and health counseling may still be recommended annually depending on your age, risk factors, and medical history. Your provider will create a personalized screening schedule.

Is a pelvic exam uncomfortable?

Most women experience mild discomfort during the speculum insertion and Pap smear, but the exam is generally brief (less than 5 minutes). Relaxing your muscles, taking deep breaths, and communicating with your provider helps minimize discomfort. If you have anxiety or previous trauma, discuss this beforehand‚Äîwe can adjust our approach to ensure your comfort.

Can I have an exam during my period?

It's best to schedule when you're not menstruating for the most accurate Pap smear results and most comfortable pelvic exam. If possible, schedule your visit midcycle (about 2 weeks after your period starts). However, if you have urgent concerns, we can still perform an exam during menstruation.

What should I do to prepare for my exam?

Avoid intercourse, douching, and vaginal medications for 48 hours before your exam, as these can affect Pap smear results. Empty your bladder before the exam for comfort. Bring a list of current medications and questions you want to discuss. No other special preparation is needed.

Will my exam include STI testing?

STI screening is recommended for sexually active women under 25 and those with risk factors (new or multiple partners). Testing isn't automatically included‚Äîyou'll need to request it or your provider will recommend it based on your history. STI tests can be added to your annual visit with minimal additional discomfort.

When will I get my test results?

Results typically arrive within 7-10 business days. We contact you by phone or secure message with all findings. If results are normal, you may receive a brief notification. Abnormal results prompt a phone call to discuss next steps, which may include repeat testing, colposcopy, or treatment.

Do I need an exam if I'm not sexually active?

Yes. Cervical cancer screening, breast exams, and overall gynecological health monitoring are important regardless of sexual activity. While some STI testing may not apply, Pap smears detect HPV-related changes that can occur through non-sexual transmission. Annual exams also address menstrual issues, hormonal health, and cancer screening.

Can I discuss birth control during my annual exam?

Absolutely. Your annual exam is the perfect opportunity to discuss contraception options, evaluate your current method, or start new birth control. We review all options‚Äîpills, IUDs, implants, injections‚Äîand help you choose the best method for your lifestyle and health needs.

What if I'm going through menopause?

Annual exams remain important through and after menopause. We screen for gynecological cancers, discuss hormone replacement therapy options, evaluate pelvic floor health, and address symptoms like hot flashes, vaginal dryness, and bone health. Menopause is a natural transition we help you navigate comfortably.

Is the exam covered by insurance?

Most insurance plans cover annual well-woman exams as preventive care with no copay. Coverage varies by plan, so verify your benefits beforehand. We accept major insurance plans and offer self-pay options. Call our Kingston office with insurance questions before your visit.
