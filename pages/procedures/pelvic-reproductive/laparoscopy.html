Overview

Laparoscopy at OSHUNJA offers minimally invasive surgery through tiny incisions, allowing our Kingston gynecologists to diagnose and treat pelvic conditions with remarkable precision. Using a thin camera and specialized instruments, we access your abdomen and pelvis without large incisions, resulting in less pain, minimal scarring, and dramatically faster recovery compared to traditional open surgery.

From removing endometriosis and ovarian cysts to performing hysterectomies and treating ectopic pregnancies, laparoscopic surgery revolutionizes gynecological care. Most patients go home the same day, return to work within a week, and experience significantly less post-operative discomfort than conventional surgery—all while achieving superior visual access for thorough treatment.

Our surgeons bring extensive laparoscopic expertise to complex procedures. As leaders in minimally invasive gynecological surgery in Jamaica, we offer advanced techniques including robotic-assisted options for optimal outcomes with minimal disruption to your life.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Pre-Operative Evaluation

Comprehensive consultation reviews medical history, imaging, and lab work. We discuss surgical goals, anesthesia options, and recovery expectations. Pre-operative testing ensures safety.

2

Anesthesia & Initial Setup

General anesthesia is administered. Your abdomen is gently inflated with carbon dioxide gas to create workspace between abdominal wall and organs, allowing safe visualization without harming internal structures.

3

Laparoscope Insertion

Small incisions (0.5-1 cm) made near navel and lower abdomen. Laparoscope—thin camera with light—inserted to display magnified view of pelvic organs on high-definition monitor. Additional ports allow specialized instruments access.

4

Surgical Procedure

Using magnified visualization and specialized instruments, we perform planned surgery: remove endometriosis, excise ovarian cysts, treat ectopic pregnancy, perform hysterectomy, or address other pelvic conditions. Precision instruments minimize blood loss and tissue trauma.

5

Closure & Recovery

Gas removed from abdomen, instruments withdrawn, and tiny incisions closed with absorbable sutures or surgical glue. Most patients go home same day after brief recovery. Scarring minimal—incisions heal to barely visible lines.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Laparoscopy May Be Right for You If:

  • You have endometriosis causing chronic pelvic pain, painful periods, or infertility requiring surgical treatment
  • Ovarian cysts persist, grow larger, cause symptoms, or appear suspicious on imaging
  • Chronic pelvic pain remains unexplained after testing—laparoscopy provides visual diagnosis
  • Ectopic pregnancy diagnosed and requires surgical intervention
  • Fibroids need removal but you want fertility-preserving laparoscopic myomectomy
  • You're considering hysterectomy and want minimally invasive approach with faster recovery
  • Pelvic adhesions from previous surgery or infection cause symptoms requiring lysis
  • Tubal ligation desired for permanent birth control

Important Considerations:

  • General Anesthesia: All laparoscopic procedures require general anesthesia—ensure you're healthy enough for surgery
  • Conversion to Open: Rarely, laparoscopy converts to open surgery if unexpected complications, severe adhesions, or poor visualization occur
  • Gas Pain: Shoulder and chest discomfort from residual CO2 gas common for 2-3 days post-operatively
  • Not Always Possible: Severe obesity, extensive previous surgeries, or certain medical conditions may make laparoscopy unsafe
  • Recovery Variability: Simple procedures recover in days; complex surgeries may take 2-4 weeks
  • Surgical Expertise: Laparoscopic skill varies significantly—ensure your surgeon has advanced training and experience
Important: While laparoscopy offers significant advantages, not all conditions are suitable for laparoscopic approach. Thorough evaluation determines best surgical method.

Recovery & Aftercare

Day of Surgery

  • Recover in facility for 2-4 hours before discharge
  • Shoulder/chest pain from residual gas is normal
  • Incision sites covered with small bandages
  • Arrange ride home—cannot drive after anesthesia
  • Rest and limit activity first 24 hours

Days 1-3

  • Gas pain peaks days 1-2, then resolves by day 3
  • Incision soreness managed with prescribed pain medication
  • Light walking encouraged to prevent blood clots
  • No lifting over 10 pounds
  • Shower permitted—keep incisions clean and dry

Week 1-2

  • Most discomfort resolved by end of week 1
  • Gradually increase activities as tolerated
  • Return to work for sedentary jobs (week 1-2)
  • Continue avoiding heavy lifting and strenuous exercise
  • Follow-up appointment to review pathology/surgical findings

Weeks 2-4

  • Resume normal exercise and physical activity
  • Sexual activity can resume (typically 2 weeks post-op)
  • Incisions fade to barely visible scars
  • Complete recovery achieved by 4 weeks for most

Aftercare Instructions

Activity: Light walking encouraged; no heavy lifting for 2 weeks
Gas Pain: Walk frequently, use heating pad on shoulders
Incisions: Keep clean and dry, remove bandages after 48 hours
Pain: Use prescribed medications as directed
Intercourse: Wait 2 weeks or until cleared by surgeon
Follow-Up: Attend post-op visit to review results
When to Contact Us: Call immediately if you experience fever over 100.4°F, severe abdominal pain, heavy vaginal bleeding, foul-smelling discharge, redness/swelling at incisions, or difficulty breathing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is laparoscopy different from open surgery?

Laparoscopy uses 3-4 tiny incisions (0.5-1 cm) and a camera for visualization, while open surgery requires one large incision (10-20 cm). Laparoscopy results in less pain, faster recovery, minimal scarring, and same-day discharge. Both achieve the same surgical goals—laparoscopy just does so with less trauma.

Why do I have shoulder pain after laparoscopy?

Carbon dioxide gas used to inflate your abdomen irritates the diaphragm, which shares nerves with your shoulder. This referred pain is completely normal and resolves within 2-3 days as your body absorbs the gas. Walking frequently helps eliminate gas faster.

Can all gynecological surgeries be done laparoscopically?

Most gynecological surgeries can be performed laparoscopically, including hysterectomy, myomectomy, ovarian cyst removal, and endometriosis treatment. However, very large uteruses, extensive cancer, or severe adhesions may require open surgery for safety. Your surgeon recommends the best approach for your situation.

How long does laparoscopic surgery take?

Duration varies by procedure complexity. Simple cyst removal takes 30-60 minutes. Laparoscopic hysterectomy takes 1-3 hours. Extensive endometriosis excision may take 3-4 hours. Your surgeon provides estimated timeframe during pre-operative consultation.

Will the scars be visible?

Laparoscopic scars are minimal. One incision hides in your navel; others are placed in lower abdomen/bikini line. Incisions heal to faint lines barely visible after several months. Most patients find scars cosmetically acceptable.

When can I return to exercise after laparoscopy?

Light walking is encouraged immediately. Resume light cardio (walking, stationary bike) after 1 week. Avoid ab work, running, and heavy lifting for 2 weeks. Full exercise including weights, high-intensity workouts, and core work can resume at 3-4 weeks post-op.

What are the risks of laparoscopy?

Risks include bleeding, infection, injury to bladder/bowel/blood vessels (rare, <1%), blood clots, anesthesia complications, and conversion to open surgery. Laparoscopy is very safe—serious complications occur in less than 1% of cases with experienced surgeons. We take extensive precautions to minimize all risks.

Is robotic surgery better than regular laparoscopy?

Robotic-assisted laparoscopy offers enhanced 3D visualization and greater instrument precision, beneficial for complex procedures like myomectomy or extensive endometriosis excision. For straightforward procedures, traditional laparoscopy is equally effective. We recommend the approach best suited to your specific condition.

Can laparoscopy diagnose endometriosis?

Yes. Laparoscopy is the gold standard for endometriosis diagnosis. We visually identify endometriosis implants and can treat them during the same surgery by excision or ablation. Imaging and exams cannot definitively diagnose endometriosis—only laparoscopy provides certain confirmation.

Is laparoscopic surgery covered by insurance?

When medically necessary (endometriosis, ovarian cysts, fibroids, chronic pain), laparoscopic surgery is typically covered by insurance, often with better coverage than open surgery due to lower hospitalization costs. Verify your specific plan benefits. Our team assists with insurance pre-authorization.
