Overview

Hysterectomy at OSHUNJA offers surgical removal of the uterus to treat conditions unresponsive to conservative therapy‚Äîincluding fibroids causing severe bleeding or pain, endometriosis, uterine prolapse, gynecological cancers, or chronic pelvic pain. Our Kingston surgeons specialize in minimally invasive approaches (laparoscopic and vaginal) that minimize scarring, reduce recovery time, and optimize outcomes compared to traditional open surgery.

This permanent procedure eliminates menstruation and prevents pregnancy, making it appropriate only after completing childbearing or when fertility preservation isn't desired. Depending on your condition, we may remove just your uterus (partial hysterectomy), uterus plus cervix (total hysterectomy), or include ovaries and fallopian tubes (complete hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy). Each approach is tailored to your specific medical needs.

While hysterectomy is major surgery, advances in minimally invasive techniques mean most women return home within 24 hours and resume normal activities within 2-4 weeks. Our experienced surgical team combines technical excellence with compassionate care, ensuring you understand every aspect of your procedure and feel confident in your decision to pursue definitive treatment.

How It Works & What To Expect

Hysterectomy at OSHUNJA begins with consultation to review your medical history, symptoms, and diagnostic needs. Our experienced Kingston gynecologists use advanced techniques and equipment to ensure accurate results and patient comfort throughout the procedure.

Depending on the specific procedure, you may receive local anesthesia, conscious sedation, or general anesthesia. We provide detailed pre- and post-procedure instructions and schedule appropriate follow-up care to address results and any ongoing treatment needs.

Benefits & Results

Hysterectomy provides definitive treatment for various gynecological conditions including fibroids, endometriosis, abnormal bleeding, or cancer. This procedure eliminates symptoms, prevents recurrence, and can be life-saving when medically indicated.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Hysterectomy candidates include women with severe gynecological conditions unresponsive to conservative treatment. You should have completed childbearing and understand the permanent nature of this procedure.

During your consultation in Kingston, we'll evaluate your medical history, discuss your specific goals, and determine if this procedure is right for your individual situation.

Recovery & Aftercare

Recovery varies by procedure and individual. Our Kingston team provides detailed aftercare instructions and is available for any questions or concerns during your recovery period.

We schedule follow-up appointments to monitor your progress and ensure optimal results.

Complementary Treatments

Hysterectomy may be combined with ovary removal, pelvic repair procedures, or other treatments based on individual medical needs. We coordinate comprehensive surgical care.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Absolutely. Our surgeons have extensive experience treating patients of all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. We use techniques specifically tailored to optimize healing for darker skin types.
