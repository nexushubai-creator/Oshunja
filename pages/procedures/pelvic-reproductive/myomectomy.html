Overview

Myomectomy at OSHUNJA surgically removes uterine fibroids while preserving your uterus and fertility. Unlike hysterectomy, this fertility-sparing procedure eliminates symptomatic fibroids causing heavy bleeding, pelvic pressure, or pregnancy complications without removing your entire reproductive organ. Our Kingston surgeons use advanced minimally invasive techniques to remove even multiple fibroids with precision.

Whether you're experiencing debilitating periods, struggling with fertility, or suffering from bladder and bowel pressure, myomectomy addresses fibroid symptoms while maintaining your ability to conceive. We offer laparoscopic, hysteroscopic, and robotic-assisted approaches tailored to your fibroid size, location, and reproductive goals‚Äîmaximizing recovery speed while optimizing future pregnancy outcomes.

As specialists in complex fibroid surgery, we've successfully treated women with multiple large fibroids who were told hysterectomy was their only option. Our expertise in fertility preservation and advanced surgical techniques gives you the best chance of symptom relief while keeping your reproductive choices open.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Comprehensive Consultation & Imaging

Your myomectomy journey begins with detailed consultation where we review your symptoms, medical history, and fertility goals. We perform comprehensive imaging (ultrasound and/or MRI) to map fibroid location, size, and number‚Äîcritical information for surgical planning. Our Kingston team evaluates fibroid characteristics to determine the optimal surgical approach for your specific situation.

2

Surgical Approach Selection

Based on your fibroid characteristics, we select the optimal approach: hysteroscopic (through the vagina for submucosal fibroids), laparoscopic (minimally invasive for intramural/subserosal fibroids), or abdominal myomectomy for large or numerous fibroids. Each approach is tailored to maximize fibroid removal while minimizing surgical trauma.

3

Fibroid Removal Procedure

Under general anesthesia, our experienced Kingston surgeons precisely locate and remove each fibroid while preserving healthy uterine tissue. Advanced techniques including careful hemostasis minimize blood loss and optimize uterine integrity for future pregnancy. We remove all visible fibroids to provide comprehensive symptom relief.

4

Uterine Reconstruction

After fibroid removal, we meticulously reconstruct your uterus using multiple-layer suturing techniques to restore structural strength‚Äîessential for successful pregnancy outcomes and preventing uterine rupture during future pregnancies.

5

Recovery & Follow-up

Post-operative monitoring ensures proper healing. Hospital stay varies by surgical approach (same-day for hysteroscopic, 1-2 nights for others). We schedule follow-up appointments to track recovery, discuss pathology results, and provide guidance on resuming activities and timing for pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I be able to get pregnant after myomectomy?

Yes! Myomectomy is specifically designed to preserve fertility. Most women can conceive after myomectomy, though we typically recommend waiting 3-6 months for complete uterine healing. Your fertility outcomes depend on factors like age, fibroid characteristics, and overall reproductive health. Many of our Kingston patients have successfully conceived and delivered healthy babies after myomectomy.

How long does recovery take?

Recovery varies by surgical approach. Hysteroscopic myomectomy: 1-2 weeks. Laparoscopic myomectomy: 2-4 weeks. Abdominal myomectomy: 4-6 weeks. Most patients return to desk work within 2 weeks and full activities by 6 weeks. Our Kingston team provides personalized recovery timelines based on your specific procedure.

Can fibroids come back after myomectomy?

Yes, fibroid recurrence is possible as myomectomy removes existing fibroids but doesn't prevent new ones from forming. Recurrence rates vary: approximately 10-30% of women develop new fibroids within 5 years. Risk factors include younger age at surgery, multiple fibroids, and genetic predisposition. However, for women wanting to preserve fertility, myomectomy remains the best option despite recurrence possibility.

What size fibroids can be removed with myomectomy?

We can remove fibroids of virtually any size through myomectomy, though surgical approach varies by size. Small fibroids (< 3 cm) are often candidates for hysteroscopic removal. Medium fibroids (3-10 cm) typically require laparoscopic approach. Large or multiple fibroids may need abdominal myomectomy. Our experienced Kingston surgeons have successfully removed fibroids exceeding 15 cm while preserving the uterus.

Is myomectomy better than hysterectomy?

"Better" depends on your goals. Myomectomy is superior if you want to preserve fertility or your uterus. It allows future pregnancy and maintains hormonal function if ovaries aren't removed. Hysterectomy eliminates fibroid recurrence risk but permanently ends fertility. For women who've completed childbearing with severe symptoms, hysterectomy may be more definitive. We'll discuss which option aligns with your personal goals during consultation.

Will I have visible scars?

Scarring depends on surgical approach. Hysteroscopic myomectomy leaves no external scars (access through vagina). Laparoscopic myomectomy creates 3-4 small (5-10mm) incisions that fade significantly. Abdominal myomectomy creates one horizontal incision at the bikini line. Our surgeons use meticulous closure techniques, and scars typically fade well‚Äîespecially important for our Caribbean patients with melanin-rich skin.

How many fibroids can be removed at once?

We can remove multiple fibroids in a single surgery‚Äîthere's no strict limit. Our Kingston team has successfully removed 10+ fibroids during one procedure. The limiting factors are surgical time, blood loss management, and maintaining uterine integrity. During pre-operative imaging, we map all fibroids and plan comprehensive removal while ensuring your safety and optimal outcomes.

What are the risks of myomectomy?

Like any surgery, myomectomy carries risks including bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding organs, and anesthesia complications. Specific to myomectomy: significant blood loss (may require transfusion), adhesion formation, uterine rupture in future pregnancy (rare), and unintended hysterectomy if complications arise (< 1%). Our experienced surgical team minimizes these risks through advanced techniques and careful patient selection.

Can I have a vaginal delivery after myomectomy?

It depends on your specific surgery. Women who had hysteroscopic or small laparoscopic myomectomies can often deliver vaginally. However, if your myomectomy involved deep uterine muscle removal or entry into the uterine cavity, cesarean delivery is usually recommended to prevent uterine rupture during labor. We'll discuss delivery planning based on your surgical details and provide documentation for your obstetric care team.

Is the procedure safe for dark skin?

Absolutely. Our surgeons have extensive experience treating patients of all skin tones, including melanin-rich Caribbean skin. We use techniques specifically tailored to optimize healing for darker skin types, minimizing keloid formation and ensuring scars fade appropriately. Women of African and Caribbean descent have excellent outcomes with proper surgical technique and post-operative care.
