Overview

Colposcopy at OSHUNJA provides detailed microscopic examination of your cervix, vagina, and vulva following abnormal Pap smear results or concerning symptoms. Using a specialized magnifying instrument (colposcope), our Kingston gynecologists identify precancerous changes, HPV-related abnormalities, and other cervical conditions invisible to the naked eye‚Äîenabling early intervention before cancer develops.

This painless in-office procedure takes 15-20 minutes and provides immediate visual diagnosis. During colposcopy, we may take small tissue samples (biopsies) from suspicious areas for laboratory analysis. Early detection through colposcopy prevents progression to invasive cervical cancer in over 90% of cases‚Äîmaking this one of the most important diagnostic tools in gynecological care.

If you received abnormal Pap results, don't panic. Most abnormalities are minor and resolve on their own or with simple treatment. Colposcopy gives us the detailed view needed to determine exactly what's happening and whether treatment is necessary. Our experienced team makes the process as comfortable as possible while providing you with clear answers and a definitive path forward.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Pre-Procedure Consultation

We review your abnormal Pap smear results, medical history, and symptoms. You'll learn exactly what to expect during colposcopy and why it's recommended. Schedule when not menstruating for optimal visualization.

2

Positioning & Preparation

You lie in standard exam position (similar to Pap smear). A speculum is inserted to visualize your cervix. The colposcope‚Äîa lighted magnifying instrument‚Äîremains outside your body, providing a detailed view.

3

Cervical Examination

We apply a mild vinegar solution to highlight abnormal areas. The colposcope magnifies your cervix 10-40 times, allowing identification of precancerous changes invisible to the naked eye. The exam itself is painless.

4

Biopsy (If Needed)

If suspicious areas are identified, small tissue samples (biopsies) are collected for laboratory analysis. You may feel brief cramping or pinching during biopsy collection. Local numbing can be applied if needed.

5

Results & Follow-Up

The examination completes in 15-20 minutes. Biopsy results arrive within 7-14 days. We contact you with findings and discuss next steps, which may include monitoring, treatment, or routine follow-up.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Colposcopy Is Recommended If:

  • Your Pap smear showed abnormal cells (ASCUS, LSIL, HSIL, or atypical glandular cells)‚Äîcolposcopy determines the severity and location
  • You tested positive for high-risk HPV strains that can cause cervical cancer, especially if combined with abnormal Pap results
  • You experience unexplained vaginal bleeding between periods or after intercourse that requires investigation
  • Your provider observed visible abnormalities on your cervix during routine pelvic exam
  • You have a history of cervical dysplasia requiring ongoing surveillance to prevent recurrence
  • Previous treatments for cervical abnormalities need follow-up evaluation to confirm complete resolution
  • You have persistent cervicitis or unusual cervical inflammation that doesn't respond to standard treatment

Important Considerations:

  • Not Diagnostic Alone: Colposcopy guides biopsies, but tissue analysis provides the definitive diagnosis of dysplasia severity
  • Timing Matters: Schedule when not menstruating for best visualization; avoid intercourse, douching, and vaginal products 48 hours before
  • Biopsy Discomfort: If biopsies are needed, you may feel brief cramping; over-the-counter pain relievers taken beforehand can help
  • Pregnancy Considerations: Colposcopy can be safely performed during pregnancy, though biopsies may be delayed depending on findings
  • Follow-Up Required: Abnormal results don't always mean cancer‚Äîmost dysplasia can be treated successfully with simple procedures
  • Don't Skip It: If your provider recommends colposcopy, it's critical for preventing cervical cancer through early intervention
Important: Abnormal Pap smears require follow-up. Delaying colposcopy allows precancerous changes to progress unnecessarily. Early detection saves lives.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does colposcopy hurt?

The colposcopy examination itself is painless‚Äîthe colposcope stays outside your body. If biopsies are needed, you may feel brief cramping or pinching similar to menstrual cramps. The discomfort lasts only seconds. Taking ibuprofen 30 minutes before your appointment can minimize cramping.

What do abnormal results mean?

Abnormal colposcopy findings indicate precancerous cellular changes (dysplasia), not cancer. Mild dysplasia often resolves on its own with monitoring. Moderate to severe dysplasia requires treatment to prevent progression to invasive cancer. Your provider will explain your specific results and recommended next steps.

How long does colposcopy take?

The entire procedure takes 15-20 minutes. The examination portion takes 5-10 minutes. If biopsies are collected, this adds a few minutes. You're in and out within 30 minutes total including check-in.

Can I drive myself home?

Yes. Colposcopy doesn't require sedation, so you can drive yourself to and from your appointment. Resume all normal activities immediately after‚Äîno restrictions on work, driving, or daily tasks.

Will I need treatment after colposcopy?

It depends on biopsy results. Normal or mildly abnormal findings may require only continued monitoring with repeat Pap smears. Moderate to severe dysplasia typically requires treatment (LEEP, cryotherapy, or cone biopsy) to remove abnormal cells before they progress.

How soon will I get biopsy results?

Biopsy results typically arrive within 7-14 days. We contact you by phone to discuss findings and next steps. If results are normal, you may receive notification by secure message. Abnormal results warrant a phone discussion with your provider.

Can I have colposcopy during pregnancy?

Yes, colposcopy is safe during pregnancy. Visual examination poses no risk. However, biopsies are typically avoided unless absolutely necessary, as pregnancy makes the cervix more vascular and prone to bleeding. Most biopsies are deferred until after delivery unless cancer is suspected.

What causes abnormal Pap smears that require colposcopy?

Most abnormal Pap smears result from HPV (human papillomavirus) infection. High-risk HPV strains can cause cellular changes that progress to cancer if untreated. Other causes include inflammation, infection, or hormonal changes. Colposcopy determines whether changes are benign or precancerous.

How often will I need follow-up colposcopy?

Follow-up frequency depends on findings. Normal results may require only routine Pap smears. Mild dysplasia typically needs colposcopy every 6-12 months until resolved. After treatment for moderate-severe dysplasia, surveillance continues for several years to ensure no recurrence.

Is colposcopy covered by insurance?

Most insurance plans cover colposcopy when medically necessary (abnormal Pap results, symptoms). It's considered diagnostic rather than screening. Verify your specific coverage beforehand. We accept major insurance plans and offer self-pay options if needed.
