Overview

Low‑intensity extracorporeal shockwave therapy (Li‑ESWT) is a proven, non‑invasive treatment for erectile dysfunction (ED) that works by restoring healthy blood flow and regenerating penile tissue. At OSHUNJA in Kingston, Jamaica, we use medical‑grade acoustic wave technology to stimulate neovascularization (new blood vessel growth), improve endothelial function, and enhance nerve sensitivity—helping you achieve stronger, more reliable erections naturally.

Unlike medications that offer temporary effects, shockwave therapy addresses one of the most common root causes of ED: reduced penile blood flow and microvascular damage. In a series of quick in‑office sessions, acoustic waves are applied to strategic areas of the penis and perineum to trigger a natural healing response and improve hemodynamics over time.

Your treatment is confidential, comfortable, and tailored to your goals. Many patients choose Li‑ESWT as a standalone solution or combine it with other regenerative options like the P‑Shot or hormone optimization for comprehensive sexual wellness.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Private Consultation

We review your medical history, current symptoms, medications, and goals. If appropriate, we may recommend labs to evaluate vascular and hormonal contributors to ED.

2

Mapping & Comfort Prep

Treatment areas are identified (corpora cavernosa, crura, penile base). A topical anesthetic may be applied for comfort—most patients report minimal discomfort.

3

Acoustic Wave Delivery

A handheld device delivers thousands of low‑intensity pulses to targeted areas over 15–20 minutes. These micro‑stresses trigger natural healing and new vessel formation.

4

Targeted Protocol Series

Most patients complete 6–12 sessions over 3–6 weeks, depending on severity and goals. Protocols may be customized if you have Peyronie’s disease or vascular risk factors.

5

Immediate Recovery

No downtime—resume normal and intimate activities the same day. Results build gradually as circulation improves and tissue remodels.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Shockwave Therapy May Be Right For You If:

  • You're experiencing ED related to blood flow (vascular insufficiency)
  • You want a natural, medication‑free approach to improving erections
  • You’ve had reduced response to PDE5 medications and want better baseline function
  • You prefer non‑invasive treatments with no downtime
  • You’re seeking performance optimization and enhanced sensitivity
  • You’re open to combining with PRP (P‑Shot) or hormone optimization for maximal results

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Contraindications: Active genital infections, open wounds, unaddressed bleeding disorders
  • Cardiovascular health: Significant unmanaged vascular disease may require coordination with your physician
  • Medications: Blood thinners can increase bruising risk (treatment may still proceed with precautions)
  • Expectations: Results are gradual and cumulative across a series of sessions
  • Peyronie’s disease: Customized protocols may help curvature and pain; discuss during consult
Note: If ED is primarily hormonal or neurogenic, additional therapies may be recommended alongside shockwave for best outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many shockwave sessions will I need?

Most patients complete 6–12 sessions over 3–6 weeks depending on baseline function and goals. We’ll personalize your protocol during consultation.

Is shockwave therapy painful?

Discomfort is typically mild and brief. A topical anesthetic may be used for sensitive areas, but most patients tolerate treatment comfortably.

When will I notice results?

Some men notice early changes within 1–2 weeks. The most significant improvements usually appear between 6–8 weeks as circulation and tissue remodeling improve.

Will I still need ED medication?

Many patients reduce or eliminate their need for medication over time. If you’re currently using PDE5 inhibitors, we’ll advise you on how to transition appropriately.

Is it safe?

Yes. Li‑ESWT is non‑invasive with an excellent safety profile when performed by trained providers. Side effects are typically mild (temporary redness or sensitivity).

Can shockwave help Peyronie’s disease?

Customized protocols may reduce discomfort and improve curvature in some cases. We’ll evaluate your case and discuss expected outcomes during consultation.

How long do results last?

Benefits can last 6–12+ months. Maintenance sessions may be recommended based on your goals and response.

Who is not a candidate?

Those with active infections, unaddressed bleeding disorders, or significant unmanaged cardiovascular disease may require alternative plans or medical clearance.
