Overview

Men’s bioidentical hormone therapy focuses on optimizing testosterone while maintaining healthy balance with estradiol and DHT. When properly prescribed and monitored, BHRT/TRT can improve energy, focus, motivation, libido, erectile function, body composition, and mood.

At OSHUNJA in Kingston, we follow evidence‑based protocols with comprehensive assessment and ongoing monitoring. We individualize dosing and delivery method (injections, gels/creams, or pellets) based on labs, symptoms, and lifestyle.

Your plan may also address sleep, metabolic health, nutrient status, and exercise—because hormones work best with a solid foundation.

We review total/free testosterone, estradiol (E2), SHBG, LH/FSH, prolactin, thyroid panel, CBC, CMP, lipids, A1C, and vitamin D, plus symptom inventory.

2

We choose the optimal route (injection, gel/cream, or pellet), dosing frequency, and adjuncts if needed (e.g., aromatase management) tailored to your goals.

Clear instructions on administration, timing, and lifestyle support. We track energy, libido, sleep, mood, and body composition markers.

4

Follow‑Up & Optimization

Re‑check targeted labs at 6–8 weeks; adjust dose to reach symptom relief without excess. We aim for physiologic ranges, not supraphysiologic peaks.

5

Maintenance & Monitoring

Ongoing visits and periodic labs maintain balance and safety. We reassess cardiovascular and hematologic markers regularly.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

Men’s BHRT May Be Right For You If:

  • You have symptoms of low T (fatigue, low libido, ED, low mood, loss of muscle)
  • Labs confirm suboptimal testosterone or abnormal hormone balance
  • You’re ready to commit to monitoring and lifestyle support
  • You seek a medical, ethical approach—not extreme dosing

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Monitoring: Regular labs (CBC, E2, lipids, A1C) and provider follow‑up are essential
  • Contraindications: Active prostate or breast cancer; uncontrolled severe sleep apnea; significant polycythemia
  • Risks: Potential for erythrocytosis, acne, hair loss predisposition, or fertility suppression—managed with protocol adjustments
  • Fertility: If family planning, we’ll discuss alternatives or adjuncts
Note: We individualize care and collaborate with your physicians when needed to prioritize safety and long‑term health.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is TRT the same as steroids?

No. Medically supervised TRT restores physiologic levels to relieve symptoms. It is not bodybuilding‑style supraphysiologic dosing.

Will TRT affect fertility?

TRT can suppress sperm production. If you’re planning children, we’ll discuss alternatives or adjuncts that preserve fertility.

Which route is best—gel, injection, or pellet?

Each has pros and cons. We select the route based on lifestyle, preference, absorption, and safety profile.

How quickly will I notice changes?

Many men notice improvements within 2–4 weeks, with continued gains through 8–12 weeks as levels stabilize.

What side effects should I watch for?

Possible effects include acne, fluid retention, mood changes, or elevated hematocrit. Regular labs help us manage and prevent issues.

Does TRT increase heart risk?

Data are mixed and depend on patient selection and monitoring. We individualize care, address risk factors, and track cardiometabolic markers.

Can TRT help my erections?

Yes. Optimizing T can improve libido and erectile quality, especially when combined with shockwave therapy or the P‑Shot as indicated.

How long will I need TRT?

Duration varies. Some men continue long‑term with monitoring; others transition off if underlying issues are corrected. We reassess regularly.
