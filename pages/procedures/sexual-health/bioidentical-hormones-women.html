Overview

Bioidentical hormone therapy (BHRT) uses hormones that are molecularly identical to those your body produces naturally. For women in perimenopause and menopause, personalized BHRT can relieve hot flashes and night sweats, improve sleep and mood, restore libido, and reduce vaginal dryness—while supporting bone, brain, and cardiovascular health.

At OSHUNJA in Kingston, we focus on safe, evidence‑based protocols tailored to your symptoms, lab results, and goals. We assess estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, thyroid function, and key metabolic markers to design a plan that addresses your whole‑person wellness.

Treatment options include creams/gels, patches, capsules, troches, and pellets. You’ll receive close follow‑up to fine‑tune dosing, ensure safety, and monitor progress over time.

How It Works & What To Expect

We review your symptoms, history, and goals. Baseline labs typically include estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, thyroid panel, vitamin D, A1C, and lipids.

We reassess symptoms and repeat targeted labs at 6–12 weeks. Doses are adjusted carefully to optimize benefits and minimize side effects.

5

Ongoing Monitoring

Regular check‑ins and periodic labs maintain balance over time. We also coordinate lifestyle and complementary therapies as needed.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

BHRT May Be Right For You If:

  • You’re experiencing perimenopause or menopause symptoms affecting daily life
  • You have low libido, vaginal dryness, or painful intimacy
  • Your sleep, energy, mood, or focus have declined with hormonal shifts
  • You want an individualized plan with medical monitoring—not one‑size‑fits‑all
  • You prefer body‑identical hormones with clear safety guidance

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Contraindications: History of estrogen‑dependent cancers, active liver disease, unexplained vaginal bleeding
  • Route matters: Transdermal estrogen may have lower clot risk vs oral; we’ll guide safest options
  • Monitoring: Regular follow‑ups and labs are essential for safety and efficacy
  • Interactions: We’ll review current meds and supplements to avoid conflicts
Note: If you have a personal or strong family history of hormone‑sensitive cancers or clotting disorders, we’ll coordinate with your physicians to determine the safest approach.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are bioidentical hormones safe?

When prescribed and monitored appropriately, BHRT is considered safe for many women. We individualize dosing, prefer safer delivery routes when indicated, and monitor regularly.

What hormones are typically used?

Common components include estradiol, progesterone, and in some cases low‑dose testosterone. Thyroid optimization may also be addressed if needed.

How soon will I feel better?

Some women notice improvements within 2–4 weeks; more comprehensive benefits typically develop over 6–12 weeks as levels stabilize.

Do I need labs before starting?

Yes. Baseline labs help tailor your plan and ensure safety. We repeat targeted labs to guide adjustments and maintain balance.

Are pellets better than creams or patches?

Each method has pros and cons. We’ll recommend a route based on your goals, convenience, and safety considerations—there’s no single “best” option.

Can BHRT increase cancer risk?

Risk depends on personal history, formulation, route, and monitoring. We use the lowest effective dose, prefer safer routes, and collaborate with your physicians as needed.

Will BHRT help with libido and vaginal dryness?

Yes, many women see improvements—especially when BHRT is combined with vaginal PRP (O‑Shot) and pelvic floor therapies like Emsella.

How long will I need BHRT?

Duration is individualized. Some continue long‑term with periodic reassessment; others taper after symptom resolution. We’ll help you decide what’s right for you.
