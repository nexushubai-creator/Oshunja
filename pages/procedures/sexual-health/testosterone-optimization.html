Overview

Testosterone optimization (TRT) helps men with clinically low or suboptimal testosterone reclaim energy, drive, libido, strength, and mental focus. At OSHUNJA in Kingston, we personalize therapy to your labs, symptoms, and goals—aiming for steady, physiologic levels rather than short‑lived peaks.

We offer multiple delivery options (injections, gels/creams, or pellets) and pair treatment with lifestyle support to enhance sleep, training, and metabolic health. Your plan is monitored closely with symptom tracking and targeted labs to ensure safety and optimal outcomes.

TRT can complement other sexual wellness therapies like Shockwave or the P‑Shot when vascular or tissue factors contribute to erectile concerns. Together, these approaches address root causes and performance.

We assess total and free testosterone, estradiol, SHBG, LH/FSH, prolactin, thyroid panel, CBC, CMP, lipids, A1C, vitamin D, and your detailed symptom profile.

Follow‑Up & Optimization

5

Maintenance & Monitoring

Periodic labs and visits maintain balance and safety. We also refine sleep, nutrition, and training strategies for sustained results.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

TRT May Be Right For You If:

  • You have symptoms of low T (fatigue, low libido, ED, low mood, poor recovery)
  • Labs confirm suboptimal testosterone or hormone imbalance
  • You seek a measured, medical approach with monitoring
  • You’re committed to lifestyle support for best results

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Contraindications: Active prostate/breast cancer, uncontrolled severe sleep apnea, significant polycythemia
  • Monitoring: Regular labs (CBC, E2, lipids, A1C) and follow‑ups are essential
  • Fertility: TRT may suppress sperm production—alternatives/adjuncts available if family planning
  • Risks: Potential acne, fluid retention, mood changes, or elevated hematocrit—managed with protocol adjustments
Note: We individualize care and collaborate with your physicians as needed to prioritize safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

What labs are needed before starting TRT?

We typically check total and free testosterone, estradiol, SHBG, LH/FSH, prolactin, thyroid panel, CBC, CMP, lipids, A1C, vitamin D, and others as indicated.

Which route is best—gels, injections, or pellets?

Each has pros/cons. We recommend based on lifestyle, absorption, convenience, and safety. There is no single best option for everyone.

Will TRT affect fertility?

TRT can suppress sperm production. If you’re planning children, we’ll discuss alternatives or adjuncts to protect fertility.

How often do I need monitoring?

We usually recheck targeted labs at 6–8 weeks, then periodically once you’re stable. We’ll tailor the schedule to your needs.

What side effects should I watch for?

Possible effects include acne, fluid retention, mood changes, irritation at application sites, or elevated hematocrit. Regular labs help us prevent and manage issues.

Does TRT increase heart risk?

Evidence varies and depends on patient selection and monitoring. We individualize care, address risk factors, and track cardiometabolic markers closely.

Can TRT improve erectile function?

Yes—optimizing T often improves libido and erectile quality, especially when combined with Shockwave or the P‑Shot if vascular factors are present.

How long will I need TRT?

Duration is individualized. Some men continue long‑term with monitoring; others transition if underlying issues are corrected. We reassess regularly.
