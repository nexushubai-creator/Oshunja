Overview

PRP Hair Restoration at OSHUNJA uses concentrated platelets from your own blood to stimulate dormant hair follicles and promote natural hair growth. This non-surgical treatment is effective for both male and female pattern hair loss, thinning hair, and early-stage baldness.

Hair loss affects confidence regardless of climate, but Kingston's sun exposure can accelerate thinning. Our PRP treatments inject growth factors directly into the scalp, awakening follicles, improving hair density, and strengthening existing strands for fuller, healthier hair.

The process involves drawing blood, isolating platelet-rich plasma, then injecting it strategically across areas of thinning or loss. Most patients see visible improvements within 3-6 months, with optimal results after a series of 3-4 treatments.

How It Works & What To Expect

1

Scalp Assessment & Blood Draw

We evaluate your hair loss pattern, scalp health, and discuss realistic expectations. A small blood sample (similar to routine lab work) is drawn and processed in a centrifuge to isolate platelet-rich plasma.

2

Scalp Preparation & Numbing

Your scalp is cleansed and local anesthetic is applied to treatment areas. We photograph baseline conditions for progress tracking. Numbing ensures comfortable treatment.

3

PRP Injections

Using fine needles, concentrated PRP is injected strategically across thinning areas and the hairline. Growth factors penetrate deep into follicles, stimulating dormant cells and improving blood flow to the scalp.

4

Post-Treatment Care

Gentle massage helps distribute PRP. You'll receive aftercare instructions and schedule your next treatment (typically 4-6 weeks apart). Most patients need 3-4 sessions for optimal results.

Ideal Candidates & Considerations

PRP Hair Restoration May Be Right for You If:

  • You have early to moderate hair loss or thinning—PRP works best when follicles are still viable, not completely dormant
  • You're experiencing male or female pattern baldness, androgenetic alopecia, or diffuse thinning
  • You want a natural, non-surgical approach before considering hair transplant surgery
  • You've tried topical treatments (Rogaine, Minoxidil) without satisfactory results
  • You're willing to commit to a series of 3-4 treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart for best outcomes
  • You have realistic expectations—PRP improves density and slows loss but won't restore completely bald areas
  • You're in good health with no blood disorders, active scalp infections, or conditions affecting healing

Important Safety Considerations:

  • Stage of Hair Loss: PRP works best for early-moderate loss with viable follicles. Completely bald areas for years won't respond—consider hair transplant instead
  • Blood Disorders: Platelet dysfunction, clotting disorders, or conditions requiring blood thinners may make PRP unsafe or ineffective
  • Scalp Conditions: Active infections, psoriasis flares, or dermatitis must be treated before PRP—can't inject into inflamed or infected scalp
  • Smoking: Nicotine constricts blood vessels and reduces PRP effectiveness. Quit or reduce smoking for optimal results
  • Realistic Timeline: Results take 3-6 months to become visible. This isn't an instant fix—growth factors need time to work
  • Maintenance Required: Results aren't permanent. Plan for touch-up treatments every 6-12 months to sustain hair growth and density
Contraindications: PRP Hair Restoration is not recommended for those with blood clotting disorders, active scalp infections, cancer, immunosuppression, or chronic liver disease. We'll assess candidacy during consultation.

Recovery & Aftercare

First 24 Hours

  • Mild scalp tenderness or tightness is normal
  • Small injection sites may be slightly red
  • Avoid washing hair for 24 hours
  • No hats, tight headbands, or pulling on hair
  • Sleep with head slightly elevated

Days 2-3

  • Resume gentle hair washing with mild shampoo
  • Tenderness subsides significantly
  • Return to work and normal activities
  • Avoid vigorous exercise for 48 hours
  • Continue avoiding harsh chemicals or treatments

First Week

  • All discomfort resolved
  • Resume normal hair care routine
  • Avoid chemical treatments (coloring, perms) for 1 week
  • Can resume all exercise and activities
  • Schedule next PRP session (4-6 weeks out)

Weeks 4-24

  • Continue treatment series for optimal results
  • Monitor progress with photos
  • Reduced shedding becomes noticeable
  • New growth begins appearing around month 3-4

Aftercare Instructions

Hair Washing: Wait 24 hours, then resume with gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
Exercise: Avoid intense workouts for 48 hours to prevent excessive sweating
Chemical Treatments: No hair color, perms, or harsh treatments for 1 week post-injection
Sun Protection: Protect scalp from direct sun in Jamaica's climate
Supplements: Consider biotin and other hair vitamins to support growth (discuss with our team)
Follow-up: Attend all sessions in your treatment series for cumulative benefits
When to Contact Us: Call if you experience severe pain, signs of scalp infection (increasing redness, warmth, pus), or unusual reactions after treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does PRP really work for hair loss?

Yes, when used for appropriate candidates. Clinical studies show PRP can increase hair density, thickness, and growth phase duration. Results are most effective for early to moderate androgenetic alopecia when follicles are still viable.

How many treatments do I need?

Most patients need 3-4 initial treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart for optimal results. After that, maintenance treatments every 6-12 months help sustain hair growth and density.

Is PRP hair treatment painful?

Most patients experience mild discomfort. We use local anesthetic to numb the scalp before injections. You'll feel pressure and some pinching sensations, but the treatment is generally well-tolerated.

When will I see results?

Initial improvements like reduced shedding appear around 1-2 months. Visible new growth typically begins at 3-4 months. Optimal results are usually evident at 6 months after completing the treatment series.

Can PRP restore completely bald areas?

Unfortunately, no. PRP works by stimulating existing follicles—it can't regenerate follicles that have been dormant for years. For advanced baldness, hair transplant surgery may be more appropriate.

Is PRP safe? Are there side effects?

Very safe—PRP uses your own blood, so there's no risk of allergic reaction or rejection. Side effects are minimal: temporary scalp tenderness, mild swelling, or small bumps at injection sites that resolve quickly.

Can women use PRP for hair loss?

Absolutely! PRP is highly effective for female pattern hair loss and diffuse thinning. Many women see excellent results, particularly when hair loss is hormonal or related to aging.

What's the difference between PRP and hair transplant?

PRP is non-surgical, uses growth factors to stimulate existing follicles, and requires maintenance. Hair transplant is surgical, physically moves follicles to bald areas, and provides permanent results. Both have different indications.

Can I combine PRP with other hair loss treatments?

Yes! PRP works well alongside Rogaine, finasteride, or hair transplant. Combination approaches often provide better results than any single treatment alone. We'll create a comprehensive plan for you.

How much does PRP hair restoration cost in Kingston?

Cost depends on treatment area size and number of sessions. During your Kingston consultation, we'll provide detailed pricing. We offer package rates for multiple sessions at a discounted rate.
